Happy Holidays 2012 2013

 

 

 

 

Happy Winter Holidays and Peaceful 2013

From Mount Vernon, only two miles from the home of our first president, George Washington, we wish you a restful and exciting holiday season and a peaceful 2013. We have been lucky this year - the weather has been good here; we escaped the wrath of Hurricane Sandy. We spent time with family and friends, enjoyed the great outdoors and took in the many cultural activities of our area. Margaret made a major change to explore new paths, Richard is in the process of writing the book he has been planning for several years and Kyle has moved closer to completing his doctoral studies.

 

As of July 1, 2012 Margaret retired from her second career school counselor. She loved her 7 years at Mount Vernon High School, helping adolescents balance academics success, personal development, and societal pressures. Since July Margaret completed her Master Gardener training and is working to complete her intern status by volunteering in various gardening education projects including turning Mount Vernon HS garden into a learning experience for students.

 

Besides volunteer gardening Margaret, with Richard s engineering assistance, is redesigning our much increased garden area. This fall we took a bold step of demolishing our swimming pool. Following the 2011 earthquake some of the pipes cracked, causing multiple leaks. Economics and sound sustainable environment thinking won out. So now with a plot plan in hand we are in the process of redesigning our garden area. What fun!

 

Margaret is also keeping involved with school counseling by helping George Washington University graduate students succeed in their internship experience. Spanish is quickly becoming the second language in our area; Margaret has continued her studies by completing intermediate college level and now reading and discussing Spanish literature in the original language. Having more time to spend with family and friends, to read and exercise has been great!

 

Richard has continued with his genealogy and south east Ohio history endeavors. One of the interesting by-products has been the interest that oil and gas companies involved with fracking for oil and gas in Ohio have shown in the land records that Richard has digitized. Those land records are very important to the companies to figure out who owns the mineral rights! Fracking has brought new life to the southeastern Ohio. The land records CDs are on the bestsellers list!

 

After years of genealogy research on the his parents family lines, Richard has put pen to paper and is actually writing the book(s). Look for completion sometime in 2013.

 

Kyle is also working hard to complete his doctoral studies. He published several papers this year, presented at conferences and continued as a research and teaching assistant at Brandeis University in Waltham, Massachusetts. He moved from Amherst to Waltham (Boston area) in August. During the summer Kyle spent several weeks at home: programming, swimming (at the time we did not know that in a few short months the pool would be gone!) and evaluating his mom s baking skills. When the time came to make the move to Boston, mom volunteered to drive up, help and enjoy 5 days in Boston. We had a lot of fun.

 

Although Richard travelled to Ohio several times, we did not make any major family trips this year but are planning to travel more next year. Margaret joined Richard for one of the trips to Ohio for the Frank Family Reunion. As usual it was fun and relaxing with great company and food and exploration of country life in one the most beautiful areas of Ohio. Margaret made one exciting trip to California to visit her grand-niece, Sienna, and help celebrate the 40th birthday of her niece, Christine.

 

We are looking forward to this year s Christmas with Kyle, Margaret s brother, John, and his wife, Connie, as well as friends joining us in Mount Vernon.

 

Wishing you and your family good health and spirit of peace,

 

 

 

Richard, Margaret and Kyle

 

Again this year we are following our tradition of sending as many of our greeting cards by way of electronic media as possible. An objective of this tradition is to channel to charities, the proceeds that would normally accrue to card manufacturers (like Hallmark which is now located in China), retailers, transportation companies, the US Postal Service, and other companies that use the holidays for their financial gains. In this tradition, we estimate the cost savings from cards, stationery, and postage and donate triple that amount to charity.

This is not an attempt to make a moral statement. Rather, it is an attempt to avoid unnecessary waste and provide resources to deserving people through charities. It saves trees, too.
