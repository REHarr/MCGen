The HODGE Family

(You can search any file or page on this web site by following the steps found by clicking on Search.)

 

Sometime during the first half of the nineteenth century two HODGE families came to Monroe County from Pennsylvania. William Hodge (about 1777 - February 20, 1871) was a farmer and appears to have arrived in the early 1840s. This estimate of the time of his arrival is based on the fact that he purchased land in Bethel Township in 1844. James Hodge (about 1778 - October 29, 1851) is believed to be the brother of William Hodge (about 1777 - February 20, 1871) and may have come to Monroe County at the same time or possibly earlier. James is listed in Malaga in the 1840 census. A William Hodge is also listed as being in Malaga in the 1840 census, but it is not clear whether this was James father, who was also named William Hodge (about 1757 - about October 1852), or his brother who subsequently purchased land in Bethel Township. In the 1850 census James was listed as being a tailor and lived in or near the town of Malaga, Ohio. At the time of the 1850 census, William was 73 years old and James was 72 years old.

Both the 1850 and 1860 census indicated that William Hodge was head of his household at ages 73 and 84 respectively. In 1850 six people are enumerated in his household: William (age 73); Martha (age 25); Mary (14); Phebe (10); William R. (11); and Samuel (24).

 

Ten years later at the 1860 census, the household was enumerated as 4 people: William (age 84); Samuel (age 38); Samuel's wife, Catharine (30) and their son, John W. Hodge (8 months). In 1860 Martha and Mary A. were enumerated as a separate household which included a third member, Luther Tippans (age 12). This second household was enumerated sequentially with that of the William Hodge household indicating that the two residences must have been close together or possibly even contiguous. It is quite possible that this may have been the census-taker=s way of showing that there were two components of this family. By the time of the 1860 census, William was apparently too old to care for the farm and Samuel and his family had effectively taken it over. William R. Hodge who would have been about 21 years old was absent from the 1860 census count.

 

Most of the records that would have contained information about the Hodge families in Monroe County were lost in the Monroe County courthouse fire of 1867. As a result, facts about the Hodge family in Monroe County are sparse. However, a lawsuit involving William Hodge, father of James Hodge (about 1778 - October 29, 1851) and probably William Hodge (about 1777 - February 20, 1871) provides considerable insight and information about the family. Information from the court records is as follows: Peter Waddle, plaintiff, of Bethel Township, Monroe County, Ohio asserted that on March 5, 1847 William Hodge, defendant, borrowed a mare which he drove from Bethel Township to Malaga [a distance of perhaps 20 miles]. Waddle said that Hodge abused the mare that then sickened and became of no value. The mare died on April 1, 1847. Waddle asked $90 damages.

On April 24, 1847 at 12:o'clock the defendant, William Hodge (about 1757 - about October 1852) appeared and disputed the plaintiff's claim and demanded a jury trial. The trial was held on May 1, 1874. Jurors included Henry Winland, Robert Martin, Jacob Miller, Sen. William Holden, Francis Miller and Jack Miller, Jr. The jury found for the plaintiff in the amount of $60 plus $34.70 up to this date, May 3. The defendant's witnesses included: Robert Gatchel, Jonathan Conner, John L. Henthorn, John Enlow, Matthew Scott, Thomas Fisher, William Hodge, James Hodge, Martha Hodge, Elisabeth Enlow, Samuel Fisher, James Enlow, James Thomson, John Moray and Samuel Hodge.

 

On May 3, 1847, apparently at the end of the trial, "James Hodge, son, and Jonathan Connor do acknowledge ourselves bail for William Hodge in the sum of $250 to be levied of our goods and chattels." This transaction occurred before Thomas Miller, a Justice of Peace for Bethel Township, Monroe County. This bail or bond identifies the defendant, William Hodge (about 1757 - about October 1852), to be the father of James Hodge (about 1778 - October 29, 1851) of Malaga, Ohio and likely the father of William Hodge (about 1777 - February 20, 1871) of Bethel Township.

 

According to the 1850 census, James Hodge (about 1778 - October 29, 1851) of Malaga was then 72 years old. This age is confirmed by his death record. At the time of the trial on May 1, 1847 he would have been 68 or 69 years old, depending on what date his birthday fell. If we assume that his father, William Hodge, had been about 20 years old when James was born, this would have made William around 88 when he borrowed the mare. The estimate of his dates of birth and death that have been used here are based on this conclusion.

 

William Hodge (about 1757 - about October 1852) never paid the court-assessed penalty. According to the court records, "William Hodge died in the October term of this Court in 1852," leaving his estate insolvent. We do not know the exact date of his death. The records indicate that James Hodge had died the year prior to his father on October 29, 1851. On November 1, 1852 Peter Waddle caused the only surviving bond-holder, Jonathan Connor, to pay the full amount of the judgment, $122.60.

 

Jonathan Connor was apparently a friend of the Hodge family. He testified for William Hodge at his trial in May of 1847 and within a day or two co-signed, with William's son, James Hodge, a bail bond for William's release. But, little did he expect that both his co-signer, James, and William Hodge would soon die leaving him to pay the full bill. Understandably, Jonathan Conner wanted to recoup his money. So on May 23, 1860 Jonathan Conner brought a civil action against the heirs of James Hodge. Jonathan Conner knew that James Hodge had owned lot number 3 adjoining the town of Malaga on the west side and that the heirs of James had sold this lot to Peter Mann for $120. Peter Mann had not yet paid for the lot, however, even though he had built on the property and paid the taxes on it. Peter Mann had deferred payment until he could get a clear title to the property. Jonathan Conner's objective with the suit was to cause the James Hodge's heirs to provide a clear title to the lot so that Peter Mann could pay for it and the money for the sale would be given to him as repayment for having to pay Peter Waddle for the bail bond.

 

The May 23, 1860 civil action, Jonathan Conner versus Hodge, specified the following as defendants: Peter Mann; Kinworthy Hodge [in California]; William Hodge who lived in Maryland; John Hodge; Robert Hodge who lived in Monroe County; Joseph Hodge who resided in Noble County; Margaret Hodge Starr who married Moses Starr; and a single daughter who lived in Guernsey County, Ohio. The Court issue a summons to the sheriff stating: "You are commanded to notify [defendants] that they have been sued by Jonathan Conner in the Court of Common Pleas of Monroe County and that unless they answer by 23 June 1860, the petition in said case as against them will be taken as true and judgment rendered accordingly " Peter Mann's answer was that he was ready to pay the $120 as soon as he got a deed. He had bought the lot from Robert Hodge on March 14, 1856 as an agent of the heirs. The case was resolved by Robert Hodge. The other heirs did not answer. Peter Mann paid the court $120 and got a clear deed. Conner got his money.
