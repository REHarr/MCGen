The birth record of each individual occupies a single line on two consecutive pages (e.g., pages 60 and 61).  For example, the birth record of John Doe begins on an even numbered page (the page on the left) with John Doe’s name.  On this even numbered page (example, page 60) are his full name, his date of birth, his place of birth, and his sex.  John Doe’s birth record continues across to the next page on the right, the odd numbered page (example, page 61).  On the odd numbered page are given his color, name of father, maiden name of mother, place of residence of parents, and by whom this birth was reported.  To locate the continuation of John Doe’s birth record on the right or odd numbered page, it may be necessary for the reader to count down the number of lines from the top of the left or even numbered page on which John Doe’s name appears.  Then, count the same number of lines down on the right or odd numbered page.  In many cases, the surname of the father on the odd numbered page will be sufficient to locate the continuation of the birth record.  However, the reader is cautioned that sometimes two different family members with the same surname will appear consecutively on the same page.  Caution is advised to assure that the two halves of the same birth record are being used
