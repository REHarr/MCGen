The Nova Scotia Harrington family was quite prolific. Of the eleven children of Charles William and Caroline Couch Willigar Harrington, only five were known to have children. However, these five were responsible for at least 42 off-spring. In my research for this book, I was unable to exhaustively explore this crowd of Harrington descendants. Like most places where modern mobility occurred, many of the Harrington descendants moved away or were otherwise lost. In the album that follows, I have chosen to offer the reader a sample of 7 consecutive generations of the Edith Mabel Harrington branch and add pictures of people from other branches as their photos were available.

 

To preserve the order of the Charles William and Caroline Couch Willigar Harrington family the material that follows is arranged in the order that Charles and Caroline s children were born beginning with Florence May Harrington (1869-1905).

 

Florence May Harrington (1869-1905)

 

Unfortunately, we have no photographs of Florence May Harrington (1869-1905). Florence married her 1st cousin, John Patrick Harrington (1873- ?), son of John Webb Harrington (1846- ?) and his wife, Nancy Anne Embree (1843- ?). John Webb Harrington was the brother of Charles William Harrington. Florence May and John Patrick Harrington married on 26 May 1896 in Amherst, Cumberland, Nova Scotia, Canada.

 

Thomas Stewart Harrington (1870-unknown)

 

Thomas Stewart Harrington (1870- ?) was undoubtedly named after his immigrant grandfather, Thomas Harrington. Unfortunately, we have no photographs of Thomas or any of his family. He first married Annie Edna Messom (1880-1903) who was born in Kings County, Nova Scotia with whom he had one daughter, Beatrice Victoria Harrington (1901-1959). Beatrice appears to have been reared by her grandparents, Fred and Hanna Munro Messom.

 

Thomas remarried with Amey Withrow after Annie s death. Amey died in 1908.

His third family had a brief history that came to a tragic end. Thomas S. Harrington's family that included his wife, Paulina (Lina) Rushton Harrington (1888-1922), and three children, Edith May Harrington (1911-1922), Caroline Harrington (1914-1922) and Thomas Stewart Harrington, Jr. (1920-1922) were lost in a house-fire on 17 April 1922 in Truro, Nova Scotia, Canada. The local newspaper, the Truro Daily News, reported the event on 18 April 1922 as follows:

 

Lives of Three Children of Mr. and Mrs. Thomas Harrington Lost in Early Morning Holocaust - Mother Seriously and Dangerously Burned.

At 5:40 o clock this morning fire alarms from boxes 17 and 15 called out the firemen to a fire in Farmers Store: corner of Prince and Dominion Streets, owned and partly occupied by Councilor J.E. Davison.
