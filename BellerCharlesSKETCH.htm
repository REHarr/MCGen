SKETCH   OF   THE   CHARLES   BELLER   FAMILY

 

This sketch was given to me in 2014 by Jim Gibbs.  It originally was written by Jean (Stollar) Beller, the wife of Charles Beller's grandson William Donald Beller; and published in a book entitled HISTORY OF TUSCARAWAS COUNTY OHIO -- 1988, available in the Tuscarawas County, Ohio library.

 

THE     CHARLES     BELLER     FAMILY

        Charles Beller was born near Stone Creek on July 22,* 1874.  As a young man he came to Dover and established Beller Transfer in about 1898.  The business was located on Second St. in the building that later became Weaver Motor Car Co., and that is now Paul's Body Shop, although at that time it fronted on Second.

       In 1899 Charles married Cora Maude (Maude C.) Miller who was born near Magnolia on April 1, 1879.  They bought a home at 726 Walnut St. and it was here that their five children were born:  Robert, Dorothy, Florence, Catherine and Ruth.  They were members of the First Moravian Church.

        In 1921 Charles bought the Alleshouse farm on Route 39 just west of Dover (the present Steele farm).  Here he operated a dairy farm -- processing the milk and delivering it to homes in Dover.  After several years he sold the transfer business to William Nash who retained the name of the business.

 

 

 

 

        Charles died on February 16, 1936 and in 1939 his wife sold the farm to James Steele and moved to 438 E. Second St., Dover where she lived until her death on July 2, 1956.

        Robert William, the eldest of Charles' five children, was born on July** 21, 1900.  He worked for his father at Beller Transfer and at Beller Dairy and also had employment at Greer Steel and National Carbon Co.  He was married to May Christner (born in Dover on May 20, 1902) on July 24, 1921.  Their son William Donald, born July 22, 1922, is a carpenter and lives at 614 Evergreen Drive, Dover with his wife (the former Jean Marie Stollar*** of Massillon) to whom he was married on October 3, 1959.  Robert died on March 4, 1980.

        Dorothy Adelaide was born on April 22,**** 1903 and was a teacher in the Dover Public Schools from 1923 to 1935.  She married Ross Wagner on August 4, 1935.  She is presently living in Birmingham, Michigan and has one daughter and one granddaughter.

        Florence Virginia was born on March 30, 1905.  She worked in the office of Beller transfer until her marriage to Clayton Kessler on June 22, 1927.  She now lives in Springfield, Ohio and has one daughter and two grandchildren.

        Catherine Sarah was born on July 25, 1910 an for several years was a teacher in the Dover Public Scvhools.  She married Ralph Gibbs on April 25, 1931 and lived in Sabina,Ohio.  She had two sons and three grandchildren.  She died on March 5, 1986 in Tavares, Florida.

        Ruth Annette was born May 3, 1913 and married Jack VonBergen on May 1, 1938.  The family lived in Dover until 1959 when they moved to Sabina.  Their two children, Charles Edward (June 9 1940) and Ruth Ann (January 13, 1945) were born in Dover.  Ruth ann lost her life in an auto accident on June 17, 1963.  Ruth died on January 24, 1979.  She had two grandchildren.

 

Submitter's Notes:  [from Betty Latta Kitchen, 2014]

        * World War I draft records and the find-a-grave web site give the birth as 21 Jul 1874.

        ** Other records give his birth as 21 Jun 1900:  Ohio, Births and Christenings Index, 1800-1962, World War I Draft Registration forms, Social Security Death Index.

        *** Jean is the author of this sketch.

        **** Both the Social Security Death Index and Dorothy's obituary give the birth date as April 11, 1903.
