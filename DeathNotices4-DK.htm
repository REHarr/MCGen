Nona Maire Hayes, sister to Nellie Hayes

Written by Susie W. Hayes after the death of her sister, Nona Marie Hayes.

 

NONA MARIE HAYES

 

It is with sorrowful hearts that we report the death of Nona Marie Hayes,

oldest daughter of Mr. and Mrs. Daniel Hayes, who departed this life

December 29, 1920 at the age 18 years, one month, 19 days. She was a darling

and a pet of teh entire family as she had been a sufferer almost her entire

life, but was never known to murmur or complain. Was not until the last few

days, that it was evident to her and friends that she was nearing the close

of life. Everything was done that human hands could do to loosen the grip

that disease had fastened up her, but to no avail.

She leaves to mourn her departure a father and mother, three sisters of the

home; Susie Hayes, Nellie Hayes, Edna Hayes, Grace Hayes, four brothers;

Truman Hayes, Irvin Hayes, Allen Hayes, David Hayes, three aunts, two uncles

on her father's side, and two aunts on her mother's side, and a hoist of

relatives and friends who are all sadly grieved, but God doeth all things

well.

Short funeral services were held at the home at 10 o'clock after which the

body was taken to the Calias M.E. Church and a brief service was held by

Rev. George Morrison. Undertaker Brubach of Summerfield in charge. There we

laid her beautiful form to rest in the Calias Cemetery to wait the

resurrection morn.

 

She dropped so like a lilly fair;

Cut down at hours of morn,

So Jesus took her to his care,

To beautify and bloom

Isn't a saint divinely bright!

To outline the sun.

 

To sing the song of glad delight

Her blameless life was woon.

Death loves a bright and shining mark,

So took one sweeter flower

Leaves our home so still and dark.

In this our darkest hour;

She left us oh so desolate

The idol of our home;

and entered in at heaven's gate,

Elysian field to roam.

 

But, oh, a dreary void she left

Non ever love can fill;

Oh, God! hearts are so bereft

Tis hard yet tis thy will.

Heaven received a shining light

When she from earth was risen.

But mother's days were turned to night

When Nona went to heaven.
