Monroe County Obituaries

1895

 

Billiter, Mrs. Sarah E. Billiter
Source:  Spirit of Democracy, January 24, 1895
     Mrs. Sarah E. Billiter, wife of Noah Billiter, living three miles southwest of Sardis, Monroe county, Ohio, was born February 2, 1849, and died at her home January 13, 1895.  She was a good woman of many parts.  She loved her husband, children and home with a wifes and mothers affection.  Industrious almost to a fault she labored in connection with her husband from a very small beginning to a competency.  In her twenty-fifth year she united with the M.E. church and gave her heart to Christ.  She was greatly afflicted for over two years.  Having been stricken with paralysis that caused her much pain, finally a painful ulcer of some character developed from which she died after four months of great suffering.  During all her affliction she trusted in God and said near the close of life that God cheered her heart with His precious love.  Her funeral was largely attended, conducted by the writer of these lines at the Locust Grove U. B. church in which cemetery she was buried.  She leaves a husband, three children, a mother, three brothers, two sisters and many friends to mourn her loss.
                         Geo. M. Wilson.

Bowen, Will Bowen

Monroe Gazette, Woodsfield, Ohio, dated May 21, 1896       (Sardis)

          Will Bowen of this place died Friday morning, May 15, of typhoid fever. His death was very unexpected to all. He was laid to rest in the Sardis cemetery Sunday. Quite a large concourse of friends followed him to his last resting place, and extend their heartfelt sympathy to his mourning friends.

 

Clegg, Richy Clegg
Source:  Spirit of Democracy, January 17, 1895
     Once more our little village (Lewisville) has been visited by that hideous monster Death.  After a brief illness of that dreaded disease, scarlet fever, we are called upon to chronicle the death of Richy, the eldest son of Mr. and Mrs. Richard Clegg, who died at Lewisville January 7, 1895, aged 12 years, 10 months, and 25 days.  Richy, whose smile and prattle gladdened the hearts of father and mother, whose little steps were watched with care, whose heart so pure was taken away.  The angel of death has taken this beautiful flower and transplanted it in the garden of heavenly bliss.  What a gloom it cast over our little village; how it grieved the hearts of Mr. and Mrs. Clegg to think that of the three children only little Verda and Stevie are left.  But this was Gods will who doeth all things well.  In shouting words he said that he was going to dwell with his two little brothers around Gods throne on high.  Grieve not, for Richy has gone to Him who said, suffer little children to come unto me, and forbid them not; for of such is the kingdom of God.  How lonely it appears in school since the vacation where we find Richy absent and his seat vacant.  He is sadly missed by teacher and scholars.  The parents have the heartfelt sympathy of the entire community in their bereavement.  Interment took place in the Bracken cemetery.  The funeral services were conducted by Rev. Mr. Shafer of Summerfield.

 

Cochran, Frank W. Cochran

Spirit of Democracy, Woodsfield, Ohio, dated December 12, 1895

          Frank W. Cochran, formerly of Clarington, this county, died suddenly at his home in Pittsburg Friday evening, November 29. His death is supposed to have been caused by eating raw oysters and drinking ice-cold mil. He was about 24 years of age. His remains were brought to Clarington for interment.

 

Current, S. P. Current

Monroe Gazette, Woodsfield, Ohio, dated August 1, 1895

          The icy hand of death has again visited our community and claimed as its victim, S. P. Current, who died Tuesday morning of last week. His illness of a few days was from typhoid fever. The immediate cause of his death was hemorrhage of the bowels. He united with the Christian church at Antioch, in May, 1895, and discharged his duty as a christian until his death. He was 30 years of age and leaves a wife and three children. Interment at Antioch, Wednesday.

          J.W.P.

 

Davies, John W. Davies
Source:  Spirit of Democracy, January 10, 1895
     Jerusalem, Ohio, December 28. The following resolutions of respect are from Jerusalem Lodge No. 609, K. of  P., of Jerusalem, Ohio, adopted and spread upon the minutes of said lodge December 27, 1894, regarding the death of brother John W. Davies who died at his home in this village of pneumonia in the 41st year of his age.
     Whereas, it has pleased the Supreme Chancellor of the universe to call John W. Davies from among his family, his relatives and his brother Knights of Pythias, therefore be it
     Resolved, that it is sad for us to part with our brother, because his earnestness and
zeal as a true and faithful Knight was always endeavoring to promote the interests and welfare of the fraternity, and that he will be cherished in grateful remembrance and his friendship, charity and benevolence greatly missed.
Resolved, that in his death our order has been deprived of a faithful and true brother,
the widow of an affectionate husband, the children a loving father and society one of its honored members:  while we bow in humble submission to the chastising mandates of the Chancelor [sic] cammander [sic]-in-chief, we feel greatly grieved that our brother has been called from among us in the hours of his usefulness.
Resolved, that this lodge extend to the family and relatives of our deceased brother our heartfelt sympathy and compassion in this their sad bereavement and with tender remembrance condole with them in their sad affliction and commend them to the loving care of the Great Chancelor [sic] above, who is too wise to err and too good to be unkind and who will keep them until the end.
     Resolved, that the charter of this lodge be draped in mourning for 30 days, that a copy of these resolutions be spread upon the minutes, a copy be sent to the afflicted family, a copy sent to the Spirit of Democracy and the Monroe Gazette for publication.
              C. B. Blackledge,
              I. B. Carleton,
              E. B. Griffith,
                     Committee.

 

Davies, John W. Davies
Source:  Spirit of Democracy, January 10, 1895
     Resolutions of Respect. 
     At a regular meeting of Dexter lodge, No. 396, I.O.O.F., Beallsville, Ohio, held December 29, 1894, a preamble and resolutions upon the death of John W. Davies was adopted as follows:
     Whereas, Death has invaded our midst and taken from among us our beloved brother John W. Davies;
     Whereas, we desire to give public expression to our sorrow with which this sad event has filled our hearts and to bear witness to our love for the deceased and our esteem of his many virtues; therefore, be it
     Resolved, by the officers and members of Dexter lodge, 396, I.O.O.F., that while our hearts are made to bleed, and our heads are bowed in sorrow by the death of our brother, we submit to the will of Him who doeth all things well;
      Resolved, that in the death of our brother we are profoundly impressed with the fact that in the midst of life we are in death, that the warm heart that throbbed for others woes has joined its kindred dust, and now lies cold in death, his soul returned to God who gave it;
     Resolved, that we extend our heartfelt sympathy to the widow and children of the deceased in whose circle death has made a vacancy, and recommend them to Him who will build up the broken heart  and comfort the disconsolate;
      Resolved, that in remembrance of our beloved brother we wear the badge of mourning and drape the charter of the lodge in mourning for thirty days;
     Resolved, that a copy of these resolutions be sent to the family of the deceased brother, a copy be spread on the minutes of the lodge and copy be sent to the Monroe Gazette and Spirit of Democracy for publication.
          W. O. Simeral,
          J. R. Keyser,
          G. A. Keepers.

 

Davies, John W. Davies
Source:  Spirit of Democracy, January 17, 1895

Dillon, Smith Dillon
Source:  Spirit of Democracy, January 3, 1895
     The body of Smith Dillon, who resided between Graysv---e and Green-----, was found Sunday morning near the residence of W. A. Delaplain on the outskirts of Graysville, where he had frozen to death.  Dillon, with two young men, Amon Briggs and Winf---d  Drum, had been in Woodsfield on Saturday and late in the evening started on foot for home, a distance of twelve or thirteen miles.  The boys state that they all got very cold and that when they reached Mr. Delaplains Dillon was unable to keep up with them and they being so cold went on without him.  This was about 12 oclock at night, and the body was found frozen stiff at 10 oclock the next day.  The boys admitted at the inquest that Dillon had bought a pint of alcohol before he left town and that they were all a little funny  Dillon was a married man, but his wife has been at the county infirmary for some time.

 

Dillon, Smith Dillon
Source:  Spirit of Democracy, January 3, 1895

O. Okey, J. P., of this place, where a post mortem was held.  Mr. Dillon was on his way home from Woodsfield and was found about a quarter of a mile from Graysville on the Greenbrier road. 

 

Dorr, F. A. Dorr

Spirit of Democracy, Woodsfield, Ohio, dated December 12, 1895

          News has been received here of the death of Dr. F. A. Dorr, at his home at Sulpher Rock, Ark. On November 29. Dr. Dorr was a brother of Mr. Simon Dorr of this place, and was born in France in 1820. He came to America when thirteen years of age, and has since lived in Ohio, Iowa, and Arkansas. His wife and six sons and one daughter survive him. Dr. Dorr was not only learned in his profession but was a fine musician and an able linguist, speaking several language fluently. He had been in poor health for a year past.

 Helbling, Barbara Helbling (nee Schneider)
Source:  Spirit of Democracy, January 10, 1895
     Barbara Helbling (nee Schneider), wife of Christian Helbling, died at her home in Woodsfield, Thursday evening January 3, after an illness of several months.  Mrs. Helbling was born in Schoenau, Rheinpflaz, May 25, 1835, and was therefore at the time of her death in her sixtieth year.  In 1853 she migrated to America, accompanied by three of her sisters, Mrs. Elizabeth Schumacher who departed this life three years ago, Miss Katharine Schneider who died twelve years ago, and Mrs. Charlotte Buckio now residing in Iowa; and eight years later another sister, Mrs. Magdalena Stoehr, also came to this country.  The deceased was married to Christian Helbling March 27, 1852, and is survived by her husband and seven children, one child having died in infancy.  The living children are Augustus who is married and lives in Kansas, Elizabeth wife of Mr. S. P. Dorr, Louis, Mary, William, Dora A. and Clara R.  Mrs. Helbling was a communion member of the German Lutheran church and always lead [sic] a christian life.  She was always held in high esteem by all who knew her.  During her long illness she bore her suffering patiently and with christian fortitude.  The funeral took place Sabbath morning, the remains being followed to the cemetery by a large concourse of friends.  The services were conducted by the Rev. W. Brucker, assisted by Rev. Alfred Walls.

Lovall, Caroline Lovall

Spirit of Democracy, Woodsfield, Ohio, dated September 19, 1895

          With sadness is becomes our duty to record that the death angel has invaded the home of Lucius and Caroline Lovall, and borne away their beloved daughter, Ella Grace, with that dreadful destroyer typhoid fever. Ella Grace Lovall died September 12, 1895, aged 15 years and 2 days. She was called upon to undergo one week of suffering which was borne without complaining. Grace was a dutiful daughter, a faithful attendant of the Sunday school, and loved best by those who knew her best. Our parting seems sad, but

Earth has no sorrow

That heaven can not heal.

Peter, Michael Peter

Source: Family records in possession of great granddaughter, Ethel (Van Metre) Dedering, Lincoln, Nebraska and obituary in THE HICKMAN [Nebraska] Enterprise, 1 Jun 1895

          BIOGRAPHICAL: (from obituary and family information) He was born in York Co. PA in 1818 and went with his parents to Guernsey Co., Ohio, in about 1826. In 1841, that being the time before railroads were in operation, and not being able to possess a horse or means to pay for his conveyance, he made a journey on foot back to his old home in Pennsylvania.  Soon after his marriage to Elizabeth Schell in Monroe Co., Ohio, in 1842, they moved to Pike Co., Ohio, and entered 80 acres of what was then known as congress land. They resided there until 1871 when they moved to Nebraska where he applied for homestead land in Buda Precinct of Lancaster Co. and remained there, farming, until his death in 1895. He was one of the oldest members of the German Congregational Church of Princeton, having donated land for the church and occasionally preaching when no other minister was available. Funeral services were held at that church. The largest concorse [sic] of people was in attendance that was ever witnessed in that locality for a funeral.

          BURIAL: Princeton Cemetery, Lancaster Co., Nebraska

Pugh, Edna O. B. (Blanche) Pugh
Source:  Spirit of Democracy, January 10, 1895
     Death of Blanche Pugh.  Last week we were called upon to chronicle the death of Vernon and Bunnie Pugh, who died at Miltonsburg, the first, December 20, 1894, the second, December 25, 1894; but now we give notice of Edna O. B., the eldest and only child of Dr. J. H. and S. J. Pugh, aged 11 years, 9 months and 5 days, who died on New Years day, 1895.
     The three children were taken away within two weeks by diphtheria.  After the death of two, it was hoped that Blanche would be spared to comfort the sad hearts of Mr. and Mrs. Pugh; but such was not the case.  Death, with his icy hand, plucked the last beautiful flower and left the home childless.  How lonely will the home be when the little steps so often heard, the little prattle so much enjoyed, the little voices so well remembered, are heard no more.
     The chairs are vacant now, the toys will remain on the shelf, the school books will be packed away, the hats and bonnets will be keepsakes and the little shoes will be looked on with tears.  You can not call them back but by faithful lives you can go where they are.  The parents have the heartfelt sympathy of the entire community in their sad bereavement.  The interment took place in the Ozark cemetery.
Five who surrounded the family board,

The Father has called the children home.

The three whore gone to rest,

Weep not for they have flown,
To their Fathers heavenly throne.

The home may lonely be,
For them no more well see,
Their memory well cherish still,
And bow to Gods own will.

Wright, Thomas Wright,  Sr.
Source:  Spirit of Democracy, January 10, 1895
     Died, at his home in Perry township, December 29, 1894, Thomas Wright, sr., aged 88 years, 2 months.  He was the oldest citizen of Perry township, of which he had been a resident for 54 years.  He was married three times; his first marriage was with Ruhama Myers, March 10, 1835.  Twelve children blessed this union, of which eight are living, and highly respected.  Ruhama died in 1859.  He afterward married Catherine Myers, one child being the fruit of this union.  Catherine died in 1862.  The third marriage was with Eleanor Ullom, who still survives her husband, and is left alone at a good old age.  He was a member of the West Union church for a period of 40 years.  He was a successful farmer and business man.  A large assembly of friends and neighbors met to pay their tribute of respect to a friend, a neighbor, and a brother.  The funeral services were conducted by the writer.  Once more we are reminded that we are passing away one by one, to stand before the judgment seat of the great, I am, where the secrets of all men will be disclosed, where things will be viewed as they are, and not as they seem to be; where the approval of God, not men, shall hear, Well done, good and faithful servant; enter thou into the joys of thy Lord.  May this joy be yours, dear reader, is the wish and prayer of your friend.
        D. W. Harkins
