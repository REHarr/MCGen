Biographical Sketch of Harry Francis Dougherty

 

Henry Francis Dougherty

Source: MONROE COUNTY [OHIO] DOUGHERTY CLAN, by James McKitrick, 1989, p 173

Harry Francis was born on 9 June 1923, in Parkersburg, West Virginia. Francis attended Jefferson Elementary School and graduated from Parkersburg High School. Harry entered the US Army during WWII seeing action in the Pacific Theater and the South Pacific. Francis served with the 11th Airborne and fought in the Phillippines, Okinawa, and Japan operations. He was with the first Airborne flown into Japan. Francis was discharged from the service in 1947. Francis chose Reba Fern Stanley as his wife, marrying her on 19 June 1947. Reba was born in Parkersburg and graduated from Parkersburg High School shortly before her wedding. Francis wanted to follow in his family's footsteps and work in the law enforcement field, spending 36 years on the Parkersburg Police Department. He became Chief of Police in 1976. He served as Chief until 1978. Reba spent a number of years working in the Ohio Valley College Library. Francis and Reba are life long members of the Church of Christ, attending the Marrtown Church.
