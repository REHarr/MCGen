STORIES OF MONROE COUNTY

(You can search any file or page on this web site by following the steps found by clicking on Search.)

Every county has its collection of stories. But, Monroe County, Ohio probably has more than its share. All are interesting and many, if not most, are untold. We have tried to capture a few of them to provide the reader with some flavor of the County at various periods of its history. Stories are about people, mostly, and the people in this collection were frequently the people who shaped and gave Monroe County its personality.

.

  • Battle of Captina
  • Big Indian
  • Buroker – the western kin of a Monroe County family
  • Calvert Connection – possible ancestry of Dent family
  • Captain John Baker Family
  • Crow Family of Greene County, PA, and Monroe County, Ohio
  • Death of John Wetzel
  • Early History of Sardis, Ohio (written in 1877)
  • Early Settlements in Monroe County
  • Early Settlers of Monroe County
  • General Arthur St. Clair a defeat by the Indians
  • German and Swiss Heritage of Monroe County, Ohio
  • Greenville Treaty of 1795
  • Henry Burke's many stories and Black history – with emphasis on the Underground Railroad
  • Johnson Brothers
  • Land Act of 1785 – establishment of the Federal Survey System
  • Life at Miltonsburg 2-Room School
  • Making of Monroe County – a brief history of how Monroe became settled
  • Monroe County Oil Boom
  • Monroe County Population – a brief account of the 200-years of Monroe County population.
  • Murder of Clyde Dierkes – in 1970 a bachelor, Clyde Dierkes, was murdered and thrown into a well near Clarington, OH
  • Murder of Johan Adam Drumm – a party of seven Indians attacked Johan Adam Drumm at his home on June 22, 1757
  • Murder of a Young Bride – a bride of 6-months murdered by husband
  • Murderer Hanged – a murderer of his young bride of 6-months tried and hanged
  • Organization of Monroe County
  • Pikes Peak or Bust – a story of the gold rush told about William Young Dent by his son, Albert Eli Dent, 1930
  • Passing of an Era – The old barn
  • Sardis, Ohio - school essay written by Mrs. Ella B. Vetter in the year of 1877
  • Sardis Murder – in a drunken rage the Barker brothers kill the saloon keeper in 1875
  • Scalps – a story behind the use of human scalps taken by Indians
  • Wetzel Family
  • William Drum
  • William Spicer – a murderer or a peaceable white living among the Indians??
  • Zane's Trace – opened Interior of Ohio
