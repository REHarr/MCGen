The narrow-gauge railroad that serviced Monroe County ran through Woodsfield, Lewisville, and Caldwell. This is a photo of the wooden trestle at the Decker Station the last station before arriving at the Lewisville Station. It was located about one mile from Lewisville. In the photo above, the house located on the other side of the trestle just to the right of the center of the photo was the home of the Ray Cronin family. Further to the right, is the small Decker Station. The building in the lower right corner of the picture is a pump-house that housed a large gas-fueled engine that provided power to several oil wells that were connected by steel cables. The man standing on the railroad tracks in the upper left corner of the picture is unknown. This photo was taken before 1930.

Photo provided by Dennis and Ruth Weber
