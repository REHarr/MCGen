The John >>>>>>> ef9da23e9f6816b69f175e37096e452723be7711 Mentz family has been dificult to trace. While there is quite a bit of information available, significant pieces of information are missing that prevent me from reconstructing the family with absolute certainty. According to the 1900 census, John immigrated in 1850. I have not yet found him on a passenger list. A John Mentz was found in the 1860 census living in Wilmington, Delaware with his wife and one child. Nobody of that name was found in West Virginia or Ohio for that year. In 1852 a John Mentz applied for citizenship in Wheeling, (West) Virginia. If this is the correct John, then it is probably not the John Mentz who was in Wilmington in 1860.

     John did ======= style='font-size:14.0pt'> John did >>>>>>> ef9da23e9f6816b69f175e37096e452723be7711 not marry Elisabeth Klebe until 1863, but the family included two older children (John and Andrew) in the 1870 census of Richland, Ohio County, West Virginia. We can assume from this that they were children from a previous marriage. John's marriage certificate states that his parents were Henry and Mary Mentz (see the International Genealogical Index entry for John). Sons John and Andrew were living together in Benwood, Marshall County, West Virginia in the 1900 census.

     It is ======= style='font-size:14.0pt'> It is >>>>>>> ef9da23e9f6816b69f175e37096e452723be7711 difficult to determine for certain the children of John and Elisabeth Klebe Mentz.
