Biographical Sketch of Caroline (Callie) Gauding Stalder

 

Callie Gauding StalderSKETCH

Source: MONROE COUNTY, OHIO A History by Theresa A. Maienknecht and Stanley B. Maienknecht, page 25

     Even though the Monroe County farmer cannot compete with his counterpart in the less hilly areas of the state, his heritage of making

a living under adverse conditions has produced a strongly

individualistic attitude that continues even today.  Changes occur in the economy and the job market ebbs and flows but the stability of Monroe County continues to be centered on the family farm.

     Dairy farming has been a vital part of the county's history from the arrival of the earliest settlers to the present-day.  During the 1800's the primary source of livelihood for most residents was agriculture and the

hills of Monroe County, especially in those areas adjacent to the Ohio River, was populated by families of German-Swiss ancestry.

      Cheese was made not only in the dairies but also in many homes where the job usually fell to the housewife.  One such person was Mrs. Callie Gauding Stalder who started making cheese as a hobby when she married and moved to a farm near Sardis around 1918.  She sold cheese to

customers throughout the states and as all cheese makers, had her own recipe that produced a unique yellow cream type cheese that she called mild limburger.  In later years her product acquired the name of half and half.  Mrs. Stalder also made her own type of Switzer cheese.  It was somewhat different from the usual Switzer in taste and size, being molded into squares weighing about forty-five pounds instead of the usual large, round cheese that often weighed nearly one hundred pounds.

      Caroline (Callie) Gauding Stalder was married to Jacob Nichlaus Stalder, Jacob the son of Christian  and Caroline (Hossman) Stalder. Christian is the son of Nicholus and Magdalena (Rothacher) Stalder.
