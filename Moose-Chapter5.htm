Chapter Five     Martha Ellen (Mattie) Hall’s Family

Martha Ellen (Mattie) Hall was born in Center Township in Monroe County in 1875. The Hall Family lived close to Woodsfield in Center Township. Her Death Cert lists her parents as John Hall and Rachel Ullom. In the Death Cert for John F(M) Hall it is stated that John’s parents were Thomas Hall and Margaret Hamilton. In Rachel’s Death Cert it states that her parents were Stephen Ullom and Nellie Mitchell. Nellie was one of the nick-names used by Ele(a)nor Ellen Mitchell.

 

1. Thomas Hall married Margaret Hamilton

 

In the 1850 census Thomas and Margaret Hall are living next to William Hamilton (b. abt.1822) and his wife Minerva (b.abt.1827) and son Sylvester (b.abt 1849). All are listed as being born in Ohio. Thomas Hall (b. abt 1826) with wife Margaret (b. abt 1824) and daughter Elizabeth b. abt 1845; son William born abt 1847 and son John born abt 1848 or 1849. Both William Hamilton and Thomas Hall have “blacksmith” listed as their occupations. In fact another person, Jesse Headly is living with Thomas Hall and he is also a blacksmith.

In 1860 Minerva Hamilton and Sylvester are in Woodsfield but William Hamilton is no longer with the family.*

 

It would appear that Margaret Hamilton Hall and William Hamilton were most likely relatives, possibly siblings.  William R. Hamilton died in 1853 according to sale of a half lot in Woodsfield in 1853. Minerva was listed as his widow92.

 

Margaret died 17 Jun 186692 and Thomas died on 13 Mar 186793 before the fire that destroyed the vital records of Monroe County. Therefore it has been difficult to track these families. The other piece of information that is interesting is that William and Minerva had a son Sylvester and John Milton Hall named a son Sylvester Hamilton Hall on the Birth Record but later he was called Charles Sylvester.

 

In the 1850 and 1860 censuses, Thomas, Margaret and all children have Ohio has their birth state. However, in John Milton Hall’s Death Cert, his daughter, Mamie Kratzer, stated that John and his parents were born in Pennsylvania.

In Federal Census data, the birth place information of John Milton Hall varies from Ohio to Pennsylvania But his parents are more often shown as being from Pa. except in one census. Rachel was born in Ohio and her parents were born in Pennsylvania50. In the Death Cert. for John Hall48 his daughter, Mayme Hall Kratzer, listed his birthplace as Pittsburg, Pa. His parents were Thomas Hall and Margaret Hamilton both born in Pa.

As mentioned earlier, the Birth Records of Monroe County listed the births of all members of John and Rachel Hall’s family with the exception of Frank V. and William Arthur. In the1900 census, David Barkus (Barkas, Barkes) is found living in West Virginia in Ohio County49. He was married to Jennie (Elizabeth J.) and living with the family were Jennie’s brother and sister.  The brother was William A. Hall, born in Ohio in Jan 1871 and the sister was Mamie Hall born in Ohio in March1884. Jennie was 21 and born in April of 1873. Other information48, 50 has proven that these are the daughters and son of John M and Rachel Hall. It appears that Jennie is Elizabeth Jane possibly nicknamed Jennie and that William A and Mamie (Mayme) Hall are also the siblings of Mattie Hall Moose. The only data that is contrary to the above conclusion is that Mamie appears in the 1900 Census in Monroe County. The census in Ohio and W. Virginia were performed 10 days apart and Mamie may have moved between the two censuses or around census time. John Milton Hall’s Death Cert and Obituary clears up this situation proving that the Mamie Hall in Wheeling, WV is his daughter, Mayme48, 50.

3. Thomas (Tommie) Hall12,19,34,, (Thomas, John M.) In the 1900 census Tommie Hall is listed as being 10 years old with a birth month and year of Feb 1890.  He is not identified after 1900 in any of the records that I’ve seen. In the birth records of Monroe County, an un-named son is born to John and Rachel Ullom Hall on 28 Feb 1890. In the death records an unnamed son died on 4 March 1890 at the age of 4 days. Yet Tommie has the same birth month and year as seen in the 1900 Census of Monroe County, Ohio. A rational possibility is that they were twins and Tommie survived and his brother did not. However, that is not evident in the recorded information in the Birth Records of Monroe County. Tommie can not be found after 1900 and could have died before 1910 but no death cert has been found. No Thomas Hall with the correct birth information has been located.
