PURPOSE:

The Monroe County Chapter of the Ohio Genealogical Society was chartered in 1984 as a chapter of the Ohio Genealogical Society. Our purpose is to promote genealogy and preserve genealogical data in our county. We share this information with others and may provide the link you are seeking.

 

MEMBERSHIP:

Dues are $7.00 Individual, $10.00 Family. Members receive the quarterly newsletter and may submit queries free of charge.

MEETINGS:

Unless otherwise noted, meetings are generally held the third Sunday of each month (except December) at 2:00 p.m. at the Monroe County District Library, 96 Home Avenue, Woodsfield (740) 472-1954. Meetings are open to the public and feature speakers on local history and genealogical topics.

THE NAVIGATOR:

Exchange information and collect data through our quarterly publication, The Navigator. The Navigator has been awarded for excellence by the Ohio Genealogical Society. Issues are mailed in January, April, July and October. An annual index of names of ancestors and other people that appeared in the Navigator throughout the current year is mailed in December. Queries are free to all members but must pertain to Monroe County ancestors.

 

RESEARCH ROOM:

Visit the Research Room in the Monroe County District Library, 96 Home Avenue, Woodsfield, 43793 -- Phone: (740) 472-1954). Our chapter maintains the collection of data on Monroe County and related areas. Donations are welcome.

Hours: Monday - Thursday, 10:00 - 8:00; Friday - Saturday 10:00 - 5:00

 

SURNAME FILE:

The Surname File is located in the Research Room at the Monroe County District Library. Surnames are also published in the newsletter. Members should submit their Monroe County surnames on 3 x 5 cards (one name per card). Each card should include the surname being researched and your name and address. Send your cards to the Monroe Chapter, OGS.

 

FIRST FAMILIES and PIONEER FAMILIES OF MONROE COUNTY:

The Monroe County Chapter of OGS honors the descendants of families who originally settled this area.

 First Families of Monroe County:

This is a department of the Monroe Chapter, OGS. It is open to any member who is a direct descendent of an individual living in Monroe County by 31 December 1830. An application detailing proof of descent must be submitted with a one-time $15 application fee.

The deadline for applications for the joining First Families in 2001 will be announced soon. The price per plate for banquet will be announced soon. The deadline for making reservations will also be announced soon.

Click here for a printable request for application to First Families of Monroe County.

 

Pioneer Families of Monroe County:

This is a new department of the Monroe Chapter, OGS. This department does not replace First Families but works in conjunction with it. Pioneer Families is open to any member who is a descendent of an individual who settled in Monroe County by 31 December 1850.

To join Pioneer Families, you must submit an application detailing proof of descent. Include documents providing proof plus a one-time $15.00 application fee. If your family is already enrolled in First Families, then the application fee for Pioneer Families is only $10.00.

The deadline for applications for the joining Pioneer Families in 2001 will be announced soon. The price per plate for banquet will be announced soon. The deadline for making reservations will also be announced soon.

Click here for a printable request for application to Pioneer Families of Monroe County.
