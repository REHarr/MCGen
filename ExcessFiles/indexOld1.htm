THE NAVIGATOR:

Exchange information and collect data through the Navigator. The quarterly publication of the Monroe County Chapter of Ohio Genealogical Society, the Navigator, has been awarded for its excellence by the Ohio Genealogical Society (MCC of OGS). Issues are mailed in January, April, July and October to all members of the MCC of OGS. An annual index of names of ancestors and other people that appeared in the Navigator throughout the current year is mailed in December. Queries may be placed in the Navigator free of charge to all members but all queries must pertain to Monroe County ancestors.

MEETINGS:

Meetings of the Monroe County Chapter of the Ohio Genealogical Society are open to the public. We welcome and encourage anyone who is interested to attend.

Unless otherwise noted, meetings are generally held the third Sunday of each month (except December) at 2:00 p.m. at the Monroe County District Library, 96 Home Avenue, Woodsfield (740) 472-1954. Meetings are open to the public and feature speakers on local history and genealogical topics.

  • May 20, 2001 -- The May meeting of the Monroe County Chapter of OGS will be held at the St. Paul's United Church of Christ on South Main street in Woodsfield, Ohio.
  • August 19, 2001 -- Noted historian and author Henry Burke will be the guest speaker at a special meeting of the Monroe County Genealogical Chapter on August 19. The chapter will meet at the Stafford Park in Stafford at 2:00 p.m. to hear Mr. Burke speak on the Underground Railroad in the town and his ancestors’ involvement in it. STORIES OF MONROE COUNTY

Monroe County, Ohio is truly one of the jewels among the counties of our nation. As truly one of the frontier counties as the nation expanded westward, Monroe County was witness to fierce struggle between the Native Americans and encroaching settlers. Stories abound from this period that would challenge our best movie industry for action, heroism, defeat, courage and progress. The County saw an oil boom that changed the culture of its people forever. In this section several of the important stories, events and informational sources are included. They help set the stage for the periods in Monroe County history in which our ancestors lived. Some are just entertaining.

CEMETERIES IN MONROE COUNTY

Among the several ongoing projects within the MCC of OGS is one aimed at identifying and inventorying all of the cemeteries and their monuments within the County. Decades of exposure to the elements has taken its toll of many monuments, particular those made of limestone that were so popular in centuries past.

FIRST FAMILIES and PIONEER FAMILIES OF MONROE COUNTY:

The Monroe County Chapter of OGS honors the descendants of families who originally settled this area.

 First Families of Monroe County:

This is a department of the Monroe Chapter, OGS. It is open to any member who is a direct descendent of an individual living in Monroe County by 31 December 1830. An application detailing proof of descent must be submitted with a one-time $15 application fee.

Details of the 2001 First Families events will be announced soon.

Click here for a printable request for application to First Families of Monroe County.

 

Pioneer Families of Monroe County:

This department does not replace First Families but works in conjunction with it. Pioneer Families is open to any member who is a descendent of an individual who settled in Monroe County by 31 December 1850.

To join Pioneer Families, you must submit an application detailing proof of descent. Include documents providing proof plus a one-time $15.00 application fee. If your family is already enrolled in First Families, then the application fee for Pioneer Families is only $10.00.

Details of the 2001 Pioneer Families events will be announced soon.

Click here for a printable request for application to Pioneer Families of Monroe County.

 

RESEARCH ROOM:

Visit the Research Room in the Monroe County District Library, 96 Home Avenue, Woodsfield, 43793 -- Phone: (740) 472-1954). Our chapter maintains the collection of data on Monroe County and related areas. Many of the books and genealogy documents in the library have been donated. Donations are always welcome.

Hours: Monday - Thursday, 10:00 - 8:00; Friday - Saturday 10:00 - 5:00

The Monroe County Chapter of the Ohio Genealogical Society maintains an impressive collection of microfilm, microfiche, and CDs for the use of our members and guests. SURNAME FILE:

The Surname File is located in the Research Room at the Monroe County District Library. Surnames are also published in the newsletter. Members should submit their Monroe County surnames on 3 x 5 cards (one name per card). Each card should include the surname being researched and your name and address. Send your cards to the Monroe Chapter, OGS.
