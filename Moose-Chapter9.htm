Chapter Nine    Additional Information on John Milton Hall and Family

 

Apparently, Martha Ellen Hall’s father, John Milton Hall, was born in Pittsburg, Pennsylvania. Marlene Moose of Woodsfield found John M Hall’s obituary and Death Cert (under John F. Hall). He had died on 25 Feb. 1933 at the age 84. His obituary is below. In the 1880 census the birth places of John and his parents is PA.

 

“ John M. Hall, aged 84 years, and a pioneer Woodsfield Citizen, passed away at his home Saturday. (The) deceased had filled a number of Village and Township Official Positions.

 

Four children survive, Monroe Hall, with whom he lived since his wife’s death recently; Vie Hall, Woodsfield, Mrs. David Barkes and Mrs Mayme Hall both of Wheeling*.

 

Funeral Services were held at the Woodsfield Church of Christ on Monday afternoon and in charge of Evangelist J.V.A. Traylor and burial was made in the Oaklawn Cemetery.”

          * Daughter, Martha Ellen Moose was overlooked

 

From John M. Hall’s death certificate, his parents were Thomas and Margaret Hamilton Hall and John had died of a cerebral hemorrhage. There was also another John M. Hall about the same age in Monroe County14,30,33 and I found information reporting that he married Rachel Ullom64 but after many hours of “frustrating” research, I found that this information was not correct. In the 185013 census, I found Thomas Hall married to Margaret living in Woodsfield, Monroe County, Ohio. He had a son, John, who was one year old. The family is found again in 1860 and now John is 12. This appeared to be the correct family. In 1850 they are also living next to the family of William Hamilton and his wife, Minerva and they have a son Sylvester. Both Thomas and William are blacksmiths. The confusing fact is that all of these people list their birthplaces as Ohio. In 1870 and 1880 Thomas and wife Margaret cannot be found in census listings. From the Death Cert on John Hall it does state that he as well as both parents were born in Pennsylvania. This death information was provided by John’s daughter, Mayme (Kratzer). John was buried in Oaklawn Cemetery on 27 February 1933. Rachel Ullom Hall died on 2 December 1930 in Woodsfield and is buried in Oaklawn Cemetery. Rachel died of cirrhosis of the liver50.

 

In the birth records of Monroe County18, one of his sons was originally named, Sylvester Hamilton… later his name is Sylvester M. in the 1900 census and also, Charles Sylvester. The name in the birth records could mean that he was to be given his mother’s maiden name but later they changed their minds. Also, William amd Minerva’s son was Sylvester Hamilton. So it is possible that William Hamilton was Margaret’s brother.
