Biographical Sketch of Lillian Plum Dougherty

 

Lillian Plum nee Dougherty

Source: MONROE COUNTY DOUGHERTY CLAN, by James McKitrick, 1989, p 173

Lillian Oela was born 2 Feb 1925. Like the other children in her family she attended Jefferson Elementary School and later graduated from Parkersburg High School. She married Charles Leslie Plum. Charles was a graduate of Freed-Hardeman College of Henderson, Tennessee. Lillian was a homemaker while her children were growing up but then went into the banking field when her children aged. She worked in the Parkersburg National Bank and later retired in 1987, from the Ohio Valley Bank,

where she was a teller. Charles started out as a minister of the Church of Christ until he decided to be a policeman. He turned out to be a "fine" cop, in the Dougherty tradition. To complete his police training he attended several universities taking courses in criminal justice. Charles died of cancer on 7 August 1976. At the time of his death he was the chief of Police of Parkersburg, West Virginia. Lillian and Charles were long time members of the Church of Christ.
