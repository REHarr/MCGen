Bylaws of the MCC of OGS

 

Article I: Name

 

The name of this organization shall be the Monroe County Chapter of the Ohio Genealogical Society.

 

Article II: Purpose

 

1. To support the objectives of the Ohio Genealogical Society, especially as they apply to

Monroe County, Ohio.

 

2. To aid in the collection, indexing and preservation of the genealogical and historical records of this county, including the publication of records compiled by the chapter and its members, with copyright if advisable.

 

3. To assist individuals in tracing their ancestry and in compiling family histories by means of lectures, provision of library and newsletter assistance.

 

Article III: Membership and Dues

 

1. Membership in this chapter shall be open to all interested persons, including residents of Monroe County with genealogical interests anywhere, and residents anywhere with

genealogical interests in Monroe County.

 

2. Annual dues shall be established by vote of the membership. Dues shall be sent to the

society's address on an annual basis.

 

3. The membership year will be from January 1 to December 31 of each year.

 

 

Article IV: Meetings

 

1. Meetings of the chapter shall be held on the third Sunday of each month unless otherwise specified by the Executive Committee. There will be no meetings in December.

 

2. Special meetings may be called by the President or by the Executive Committee. Members present at any meeting for which notice has been given to all members shall constitute a quorum. When elections are to be held, provision shall be made in advance for a Nominating Committee.

 

Article V: Officers

 

1. The officers of this chapter shall be: President; Vice President, Treasurer, Recording

Secretary, Corresponding Secretary and Trustees who shall be elected every November and shall take office in January of the following year. Three trustees shall be elected for three-year terms on a staggered basis so that one new trustee shall be elected each year.

 

2. The elected officers shall constitute the Executive Committee and shall manage the affairs of the chapter between meetings.

 

3. A member of the chapter who is an officer or trustee of the Ohio Genealogical Society shall be an ex officio member of the Executive Committee.

 

4. In the event of a vacancy in any office, the Executive Committee shall appoint a suitable member to fill the vacancy for the remainder of the term.

 

5. If an officer fails to fulfill his/her duties of office, the Executive Committee will choose

another to fill the office and complete the term.

 

Article VI: Duties of Officers

 

1. The President shall preside over all meetings, appoint special committees as necessary, and carry on any other duties connected with the office. He/she will become a member of the Ohio Genealogical Society as required by the Bylaws and Standing Rules of the OGS. Said dues shall be paid by the Monroe County Chapter of the Ohio Genealogical Society.

 

2. The Vice President shall assist the President in carrying out the duties of that office and shall work under his/her direction. He/she shall serve as President in the absence of the President.

 

3. The Treasurer shall have charge of all monies of the chapter, collect all dues, book sale

revenues and any other monies payable to the chapter and pay all bills. He/she shall report

thereon at each meeting.

 

4. The Recording Secretary shall record the attendance at all the meetings and take minutes of the proceedings of all meetings.

 

5. The Corresponding Secretary shall process all incoming mail and disperse same to the proper officer or committee chairperson.

 

6. The Trustees shall assist the chapter in determining policy, establishing financial goals and doing all other duties they believe to be in the best interests of the chapter. They shall hold a minimum of one Trustees Meeting each year.

 

Article VII: Committees

 

1. The Executive Committee shall consist of those mentioned in Article V, Section 2 and shall direct the working of the society, shall carry out the policies of the Society, and shall carry on the purpose of the Society.

 

2. The Nominating Committee shall be appointed by the President at the October meeting of every year and shall present a slate of officers at the November meeting.

 

3. Chairpersons of the Standing Committees as shown in the Standing Rules shall be selected by the incoming President at the January meeting of every year.

 

Article VIII: Newsletter

 

1. The chapter shall publish a quarterly genealogical newsletter which shall be sent to all

members. The printing of queries without charge shall be limited to chapter members.

 

2. When for any reason it is required that notice be given to the full membership, such notice shall be given by publication in the newsletter.

 

Article IX: Purchasing

 

1. The Executive Committee shall establish an annual budget for all Standing Committees, as well as establishing a maximum amount which may be made for a purchase without prior approval. Said budgets and maximum amounts shall be reported at a monthly meeting to be approved by a vote of the membership present at the meeting.

 

2. Purchases of sundry supplies may be made by Executive Committee members and other chairpersons, provided that an accounting is given at the next monthly meeting.

 

3. Purchasing of major acquisitions shall be made by a vote of the membership present at a meeting.

 

Article X: Amendments

 

This constitution may be amended by a two-thirds vote of the members present at any meeting of the chapter, provided notice of the proposed amendment has been given to all members, either at a previous meeting or through a prior issue of the newsletter.

 

Article XI: Parliamentary Procedure

 

Robert's Rules of Order, Revised, when not in conflict with this Constitution and By Laws and Standing Rules, shall govern the proceedings of this organization.

 

Article XII: Tax Exemption

 

1. This chapter is organized exclusively for the purpose set forth in Article II, subject to the provisions of Section 501(c)(3) of the Internal Revenue Service Code.

 

2. This chapter will not carry on any activities not permitted to be carried on by an organization with tax exemption under Section 501(c)(3).

 

Article XIII: Dissolution

 

Upon dissolution of this chapter, any remaining assets will be distributed to another organization that is tax exempt under Section 501(c)(3).

 

Adopted May 1, 1988
