First Families of Monroe County ------------------------ Pioneer Families of Monroe County

 

The southeastern corner of Ohio that was eventually organized into Monroe County in 1813 had been largely by-passed by the pioneers for several years.  Ohio had become a state ten years earlier, in 1803, with much of the settlement being in the southwestern and central parts of the state.  The southeastern corner including the future site of Monroe County was considered less hospitable because of the hilly terrain, the lack of good farming land and because it lay on the warpath used by the Northern Ohio Indians on their way to and from Virginia.

 

Nevertheless, beginning in the late 1700s a few brave settlers staked their future and their very lives to begin to tame this wild area.  In about 1805 the number of settlers had grown to a few dozen.  These early settlers were largely people who had emigrated from Virginia and the other eastern colonies. Beginning in the decade between 1810 to 1820 the new settlers to the area were immigrants from Europe.  They came in great numbers between about 1820 through 1850. By 1830 most of the County had been explored and was rapidly being settled.

 

These early settlers and pioneers were our ancestors.  Almost everyone with family ties to the Monroe County area can find links back to one or more of these brave people.  The history of our early Monroe County ancestors is treated in more depth in other sections of this site. 

 

The Monroe County Chapter of the Ohio Genealogy Society (MCC of OGS) has established two organizational groups to honor these early ancestors and their progeny.  The First Families of Monroe County include families and individuals who are the descendants of residents of Ohio on or prior to December 31, 1830.  A second category is the Pioneer Families of Monroe County, Ohio who can trace their ancestors as residents of Monroe County, Ohio on and prior to December 31, 1850.
