The Monroe County, Ohio Scrap Book

 

        In her brief autobiography, Lena Louise Kahrig Pettit tells about becoming aware of her extensive family in Monroe County, Ohio after attending her grandmothers funeral and experiencing throngs of people whom she had never before seen.  Her curiosity about these strangers and many other members of her previously unknown family lines led her to the discipline of genealogy research and into the thickets of civil and church records and many other sources of information.  In the years that followed, Lena spent a significant portion of her life doing research, recording data and information, and writing the results of her findings.  Lena, no doubt, was partly responsible for the well-used appearance of many of the Monroe County, Ohio record books.

 

Lenas insatiable quest to discover the extent of her family lines in Monroe County eventually led her to the conclusion that she was somehow related to a large percentage of the County residents  likely, over 50% she would say.  She often said that if she walked or drove in towns and villages where she was born and reared, she felt sure that most of the people that she passed were somehow related.  Most of the people she already knew but just for fun she would sometimes engage in conversation with someone whom she did not know and innocently let the conversation drift to elicit a surname or two.  It took only another question or two to determine her relationship to the new acquaintance.

 

Lena lived a significant part of her life outside the boundaries of her beloved Monroe County and gained valuable in-depth perspective and understanding about the genealogy of the County that went well beyond that of local genealogists.  These attributes were clear to those who knew her and showed in the progression of her writings.  Her perspective on Monroe County life, relationships and genealogy challenged her to undertake a monumental task that she was destined not to finish.  Her challenge was to create a comprehensive record of the significant statistics, events and relationships of all of Monroe County, Ohio that covered its entire 200+ year history.  She called her creation, The Monroe County, Ohio Scrap Book. 

 

Lena understood that The Monroe County, Ohio Scrap Book could never be completed.  The volume of material that needed to be processed would likely require more than one lifetimes to address.  But, what was she to do?  With no interest from local researchers or genealogy groups, Lena determined to tackle the project by organizing it in a fashion that it would be a living document that could be added to as the interest arose.

 

Lenas The Monroe County, Ohio Scrap Book was on her drawing board and well underway when she was unexpectedly cut short of her lofty goal in 2004  2005.  The organization of the book was cast.  The Monroe County, Ohio Scrap Book would be a multi-volume reference book series.  Individual Volumes could be published as they became available.  The chapters of the reference book series that would be published as individual Volumes, had been defined and many years of hard work, data and information had been poured into each of them.  Chapters or Volumes were designed to accommodate a large influx of new material as it became available.  Chapters could also be sub-divided, as necessary, in subsequent editions.

 

Lenas foresight, hard work, and expertise is a credit to the County of her birth and the home that she loved.  Lena would be happy and excited to know that her hard work will not be lost with her passing but will be available to researchers for years to come.  It is with deep respect that her family and many friends have chosen this way to help preserve the memory of this brilliant and talented genealogist and author.

A Work in Progress

 

The above volumes are the actual volumes that Lena was constructing at the time of her untimely demise.  The combined size of the above volumes is about 20 megabytes.  This is large for a website but we can put them on the website, for now.  A problem may occur when a viewer who is working with an older computer, with a small RAM, and dial-up access, tries to access or download such large files.  It can take so long that some viewers may decide they cannot be downloaded or accessed.  If you are working with such a computer, just be patient and they should be available.

 

Each Volume (above) is now linked to the actual material Lena had incorporated into them.  Nothing has been done in the way of cleaning up any of the material in these volumes.  That can wait until we get around to polishing.  As of now, the site is a work in progress.
