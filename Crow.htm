1. Jacob CROW \GROH was born in 1732 in Germany. He died in Jul 1823 or Aug 1823 at the age of 91 in Greene County, Pennsylvania. He was buried about Aug 1823 in Crow Family Cemetery, Greene County, Pennsylvania. Jacob signed a will on 19 Aug 1823 in Greene County, Pennsylvania. Will Book 1, page 228, file #408

name appears to have been Groh/Gro and Americanized into Crow. The history of Jacob Crow is recorded by J. Homer Crow in "The Fireside Stories of the Jacob Crow Family." He adds the family story that Jacob lived with two sisters and his mother before migrating to America. Another story claims Jacob was the son of George Philip Groh and came to America with him in 1752.

The murders of his children are recorded in several Western Virginia and southwestern Pennsylvania history books. John Crow was killed by Indians in 1789 on Crows Run, Wetzel County, West Virginia, while on a hunting trip with older brothers, Fred and Martin. On 1 May 1791, four of Jacob's daughters, Lizabeth, Susan, Katherine and Christena, were attacked by two Indians and a white man named Spicer. Three of the sisters were tomahawked and scalped. Christena escaped, but carried the mark of a tomahawk on her neck for the rest of her life.

Susanna SEIGRIST, daughter of Johann SEIGRIST and Anna WILDERGER, was born in 1742 in Rafz, Eglisau, Canton Schaffhausen, Switzerland. She died after 1821 at the age of 79 in Greene County, Pennsylvania.

129. Susanna CROW was born on 1 Jan 1815 in Stulls Run, Marshall, West Virginia. She died on 24 Mar 1912 at the age of 97 in Glendale, Marshall, West Virginia.10

Mrs. Standiford dies at 97
Aged member of Pioneer Family-Funeral Tuesday
Mrs. Susanna Standiford, widow of Abraham Standiford, died at her home in Glendale Sunday night at eight fifteen o'clock. Funeral will be at the Christian church Tuesday at 2 p.m. They will leave the house at one o'clock promptly. She was born on Stulls Run on the 15th of January, 1815, one week after the battle of New Orleans and was in her ninety seventh year.
Mrs. Standiford usually enjoyed good health till the last few years she had been afflicted with rheumatism and other afflictions incident to old age. She has been a very active woman and possessed largely of that type of industry that could not endure inactivity or idleness and consequently always kept diligently at work at something, not as a necessity, but as a choice, believing, probably, that busy hands made a more agreeable temper. Until the past few years she did considerable work as a means of healthful exercise. She spent her life in this county on a farm and could give many accounts of rural life since her first recollection of life as it was in the early part of the past century. She has seen the introduction of many conveniences for house work as
well as labor saving machinery for the farm. In her early years sewing machines were not thought of. Almost all the wares of the family were home made and she, no doubt was one of the expert workers in that line, as many of girls of that day prided themselves in their skill at such work.
Of her children, there are living William Standiford with whom she lived, age seventy-two, Pete Standiford, age 70, Mrs. B.L. Crow, age about 68 and Mrs. Margaret Noffsinger of Parkersburg. Her daughters Mrs. Susan Garner age 72 and Mrs. Standiford died within the last three months.
She was the daughter of Peter Crow who was the son of Jacob Crow Sr. who was one of the first settlers on the south fork of Wheeling Creek, now generally called Crow's Creek and on some maps called Dunkard's Fork of Wheeling Creek. Moundsville Echo 24 Mar 1912
