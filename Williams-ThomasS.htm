And from Terry Howard (TCH6535@@aol.com)

 

Relationship from James to Samuel is not definitely proven.  But there appears to be enough evidence to make this assumption.  For one, Samuel is the same age as the Samuel, s/o James in the 1850 census.  Samuel lived in Perry Township, which is where James had settled and lived in 1850.  Family tradition claims a family relationship between Samuel and William, s/o James.  William T. Hays of Pleasants & Tyler Co. "waded across the Ohio River with his family to visit his family in Antioch."  Samuel lived near Antioch.

 

 1850 census of Monroe County, Ohio:

 

James Hays 50 VA, Susanna 35 PA, Samuel 14 PA, Isaac 12, Sarah 9, Phebee 5, William 3, Francis 3/12.

 

 James Hays 60 VA, living in the home of Isaac and Nancy Phillips of Perry Township in the 1860 census appears to be the same James.   Nancy Hays Phillips was the sister of James Hays.  Her ancestry was recorded in notes left by her daughter.

 

 Susanna Williams Hays and some of the children were living in Washington County, Ohio in 1860. Susanna daughter Sarah Salsberry, Phebe and Francis were living in separate homes.  Samuel and William are unaccounted for in the 1860 census.  Isaac Hays was living next to Levi Williams in Wayne Township. Monroe Co. OH.  Susanna later moved to Wayne Township.

 

 According to a biography of William T. Hayes in West Virginia Soldiers and Statesmen, Williams father James Hays was a Black Hawk War Veteran.  I have been unable to find any records to verify this.

Source: The Spirit of Democracy, dated, Thursday, August 21, 1902

 

Last Friday, August 15, 1902, Jacob Parry Highman, John Gatten and Harry Highman of Edwina were squirrel hunting. In some accidental manner Gatten discharged his gun, the ball entering Jacob Highman's stomach, passed through his body and out at the small of his back. The ball struck Harry Highman's hat, but did not injure him. Highman was taken home and Dr. A.F. Latta of

Graysville and Dr. F.C. Huth of Woodsfield were summoned but the wound was of such nature as to defy all medical skill and the unfortunate young man died Friday night.

 

Deceased was a son of Mr. and Mrs. Duncan Highman and was fifteen years of age. He leaves father, mother and other relatives and friends to mourn his departure. The funeral took place Saturday. Interment at the Conner church cemetery.
