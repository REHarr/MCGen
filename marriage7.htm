Members of MCC of OGS and visitors to this site have submitted a variety of announcements such as marriages, births, special events, reunions, etc. that were important to families and individuals of the period. Items submitted are posted as they are received, by the year of their occurrence. There are several ways that you can locate items of possible interest. First, you can use the alphabetical index of names below. Second, if you know the year of the event you can use the Year index. Third, you can use the "Search" feature to locate specific names or dates or events. Click here for information on how to Search this and other files on this site. You can return to this page by selecting "Marriages, etc" at the end of the Search page. To post an event on this site send it by e-mail to Richard Harrington.

Matz, Wilfred & Dortha (Kahrig) Matz
Source--Monroe County Beacon    Woodsfield, Ohio
Published--April 12, 2001 50th Anniversary
    Wilfred and Dortha (Kahrig) Matz, 150 E. Henry St. Dalton, celebrated their 50th wedding anniversary on March 25. They were married in 1951 at St. Peter's Evangelical Church, Lewisville, by Rev. George Varns.
    They are the parents of Donnie (Fran) Matz, Dalton, Linda (Donis) Kirk, Orville, Tim (Jackie)Matz, London, Carol (Blaine) Immel, Wooster; five grandchildren complete the family.
    "Whitey" retired from K & B Transport, Inc., in 1997. He enjoys golfing and his computer.
    "Dot" is employed as manager of The Dalton Dari-Ette. She enjoys working, cleaning and spending time with her family, especially the grandchildren.
     The couple celebrated their anniversary with family on March 31 at the Town-Country Resturant in Wooster.

Marriage

Source: Marietta Times:
Sams-Bach
Catherine Diane Sams and Jason Erik Bach exchanged marriage vows during an open church ceremony at 3:30 p.m. Aug. 31, 2002, at Gilman United Methodist Church in Marietta. Max Winland, the bridegroom's uncle, officiated for the double ring ceremony. The bride is the daughter of Richard and Diana Sams of Marietta. The bridegroom is the son of Kenneth and Marcia Bach of Lewisville, Ohio. He is the grandson of Laura Bach. Wedding selections were provided by Norma Dennis, organist. The bride was presented in marriage by Rick Sams, her brother. She wore an A-line gown with metallic silver embroidery that featured double spaghetti straps on the shoulders. Her headpiece was a silver tiara with mini pearls and crystals. The bride carried a nosegay cluster of purple and white stock, purple lisianthus, white stephanotis, white freesia and ruscus foliage. Maid of honor was Christine Buchanan. Bridesmaids were Jennifer Schenkel, Mary Ann Ware, Deanna Martindale and Tina Berga. They wore platinum A-line gowns with spaghetti straps and carried nosegay clusters of purple and white stock and ruscus foliage. Flower girl was Jenna Cowgill, the bride's cousin. She carried a Longaberger basket designed with a fresh arrangement to match the bridal attendants'
bouquets. Best man was Jason Hummel. Groomsmen were David Lamatrice, Tony Martin, Brian Martindale and Mike Blagg.
Ringbearer was Tyler Wise, the bride's cousin; and Claye Hummel. Guests were registered by Melissa Pracht. A reception followed at the Comfort Inn of Marietta. The bride is a 1994 graduate of Marietta High School, a 1999 graduate of Marietta College with a B.S. degree in sports medicine, and a 2002 graduate of Marshall University with a master's of science degree in exercise science. She is an exercise physiologist at Charleston Area Medical Center. The bridegroom is a 1993 graduate of Skyvue High School and a 1997 graduate of Marietta College with a B.S. degree in geology. He is a drilling engineer at Columbia Natural Resources. After a wedding trip to Aruba, the couple are residing in Teays Valley, W.Va.

102nd Birthday

James "Frank" Ricer

James Franklin "Frank" Ricer will celebrate his 102nd birthday today( July 8, 2005)

Kowalczk, Mr. and Mrs. John Kowalczk - 63rd   Wedding Anniversary
Source--Times LeaderMartins Ferry, Ohio Published--September 12, 2005

Woodsfield -- Mr. and Mrs. John Kowalczk Jr. are celebrating their 63rd wedding anniversary September 12. They were united in marriage September 12, 1942 by the late Rev. Andrew Wilczek of the Sacred Heart of Mary Catholic Church, Weirton. Kowalczyk is the son of the late John and Czeslawa Kowalczuk Sr. He served in the Army, Army Air Corps, Antiaircraft Artillery
during World War II and Air Force of the Korean War assigned to bomber base Mildenhall, England. He was awarded an Air Medal with oak leaf cluster and a European Ribbon with four battle stars. He retired as lieutenant colonel from the military and as fire inspector from the former Cosolidated Aluminum Corp., Hannibal. His wife, Angela, is the daughter of the late Anthony and Sophia Haraczy. She was an employee of the C&P Telephone Company, Weirton, and a sales clerk in the children's department of the former Stone & Thomas store of Wheeling. They are parents of a son, Michael A. of Windham, Conn. and two daughters, Joan A. of Wheeling and Denise K. Anderson, Lakeland, Florida. They have two grandsons and a great-granddaughter.
