1890 Veterans Census of Monroe County, Ohio

Copied by Shirley Harmon

 

Along with the 1890 census, schedules were made of Union Civil War veterans or their widows.  These are the Special Schedules of the Eleventh Census (1890) Enumerating Union Veterans and Widows of Union Veterans of the Civil War.  The 1890 census for Monroe Co., OH was destroyed, but the special schedule exists, and has been transcribed in this publication.  The tran­scription was made from a microfilm copy from the National Archives, housed in the Family History Library, Salt Lake City, UT (FHL Film # 338232).

 

Monroe County, Ohio, Supervisor's District No. 7, consists of Enumeration Districts (ED) 143 through ED 161.  Each ED was written by a different indi­vidual whose handwriting was often difficult to read.  The transcription has been done as faithfully as possible, even when there were obvious errors.  However, some information was probably misread due to the diffi­culty of reading the writing.  Some names or other information were checked against the 1870 and 1880 census for Monroe County and against the 12 volume set of books listing those who served in the Civil War from Ohio.

 

The 1890 special census includes the following information: House No., Family No., Names of surviving soldiers, sailors, & marines, and widows; Rank; Company; Name of regiment or vessel; Date of enlistment; Date of discharge; Length of service; Post-office address; Disability incurred; Remarks.  The house No. & family No. and information about injuries were not transcribed.  The 1890 special census was enumerated in June, 1890.

 

The information transcribed in this publication is: Name of veteran & widow; Rank; Company, Name of regiment; Date of enlistment-date of dis­charge; Length of service; Post-office address; selected remarks.

 

ABBREVIATIONS:  Pri, Private; Corp, Corporal; Sergt, Sergeant; Qm, Quartermaster; Lieut, Lieutenant; Capt, Captain; OVI, Ohio Volunteer Infantry; I, Inf, Infantry; V, Volunteer; L, Light; H, Heavy; A, Art, Artillery; C, Cav, Cavalry; y, year; m, month; d, day; wid/o, widow of; frm, former; W VA, West Virginia; co, company; ..., unreadable

 

Dates: Month first, then day, then year (18--)
