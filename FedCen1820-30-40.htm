1820-30-40 Federal Census of Monroe County

 

Although a Federal Census was required every 10 years, it was not until 1850 that the names of everyone in the household was included in the census report. Prior to 1850 only the name of the head of the household was recorded and the rest of the family was simply counted and the number of family members recorded. Nevertheless, these earlier Federal Census contain very valuable information for the genealogy researcher. Of greatest value, of course, is the ability to locate an ancestor by state or territory. These census records also include age groupings and Revolutionary pensioners.

This reference combines the 1820, 1830, and 1840 census for Monroe County, Ohio. For the convenience of our members and visitors, the index for this book is reproduced below.
