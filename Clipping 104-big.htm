September 24, 1936

DEATH

H. P. Gillespie

Source: Spirit of Democracy

 

     Dr. H. P. Gillespie, aged 69 years, passed away at his home on North Main Street at 9:00 o'clock Wednesday morning. He has been failing health for some time but was able to be out in an automobile on Tuesday. He suffered a heart attack on Wednesday morning, which resulted in his death.

     He is survived by his wife, one daughter, Mrs. A. R. Burkhart of Eastern Avenue and two sons, Dr. Byron Gillespie of North Main Street and Dr. Emerson Gillespie of Canton, Ohio; five grandchildren; two brothers Reverend Hugh Gillespie of Cincinnati and T. T. Gillespie of Woodsfield Route 4; and three sisters, Mrs. R. V. Boyd of Newark, Ohio; Mrs. W. A. Cronin of Paul Street and Mrs. Charles Norris of Woodsfield Route 4. A number of other relatives also survive.

     Dr. Gillespie was a highly respected citizen of Woodsfield having resided here for the past several years. Through his profession he contacted many people there, making scores of friends who are saddened to learn of his death.

     The body was removed to the Bauer, Willison and Turner Funeral Home where it was prepared for burial and on Wednesday evening was returned to the family home. Funeral services will be held at 1:00 o'clock Friday afternoon at the home in charge of Reverend Henry Fox, pastor of St. Paul's Evangelical Church and burial will be made in Oaklawn Cemetery

     Dr. Gillespie came to Woodsfield from Lebanon about twenty years ago and in both communities was a very successful physician, due not only to natural ability, but to close application to his profession.

March 4, 1931

BASKETBALL TOURNAMENT

Source: Spirit of Democracy

 

     Lewisville citizens and school officials, who are justly proud of their new high school building and its equipment, were hosts to hundreds of school athletic fans from all parts of the county last Friday and Saturday at the fourth annual tournament. The large attendance, keen competition and great interest showed what a hold this strenuous athletic sport has on every community.

     The Calais boys won the tournament in a clean-cut victory over Clarington, in a score of 52 to 34. Last year they were close contenders and evidently made up their minds to win this year. They did it with excellent team-work and in a spirit of fine sportsmanship gratifying to the heart of all lovers of fair play and good game.

     Sardis girls won also over Clarington and thereby maintained their high winning average of the past several years. The final score being 35 to 13.

     While team-work was fine, there were outstanding individual achievements, notably that of Miss Dorothy Hedinger of Sardis who had 28 field goals, 10 fouls and led the scoring in the entire tournament.

     Gene Smith of Clarington, was also outstanding making 22 field goals and 3 fouls.

     Principal Myron Harmon of the Lewisville Schools is to be congratulated on the hospitable treatment of visitors and  capable management of the interesting event.
