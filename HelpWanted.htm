The Monroe County Chapter of the Ohio Genealogical Society (MCC of OGS) is a very busy and exciting place! And, we love it!! We would like to be even busier because the more that we can accomplish the more resources will be available for our members and visitors. We need your help!

One of the major benefits of being a member of MCC of OGS is that by working together we can accomplish so much more than as individual researchers. Everyone benefits. Just imagine how little progress would be made if we each had to do ALL of the basic research to construct our family trees. But, with each of us pooling the information we find and sharing such efforts as compiling facts, taking inventory of cemeteries local to us, compiling surname lists, sharing family tree documents, and such, our search is much easier and a lot more fun.

All of these tasks can be a lot of fun; particularly, when we work together. Almost any of us can choose the area we would like to play in and the way we go about it. Some of us like to work in groups -- some prefer to work separately. There is something for everyone.

You do not need a computer to help. For some jobs it is a big help; for others it is not. But, for much of he original research it is not necessary. Also, you do not have to live in Monroe County to participate. You do not even have to live in the USA. Many of the projects can be done anywhere -- many without ever leaving your own home.

In the list below, several activities have been identified and the kinds of help needed is discussed. If you see something that you can do or that particularly interests you, please volunteer. At the moment we have a lot of things that we are trying to accomplish and very few volunteers to make them happen. Really, it is a lot of fun and EVERYONE can help. Please look over the list below and pick one or several things that you would like to do. Give Karen Romick a call at (phone: (740) 483-1481), drop her a letter or post card at the Monroe County Genealogical Society, P.O. Box 641, Woodsfield, OH 43793-0641, or send her an e-mail at Karen Romick. Tell her what you would like to work on and how you would like to help. Karen would love to work with you.

One more thing -- the project that you are interested in doing or the thing that needs to be done may not be on the list below. If you think of something that should be done and that would contribute to the body of information and tools of the Chapter, suggest it. Just remember that you may be the best qualified to define the specific project that you wish to undertake. This is particularly true when it comes to the things you know best -- your own family; the history of the area where you live; the cemeteries near your home; and the things that relate to your work.

Go ahead! Take the plunge -- contact Karen. You will not regret it. Karen can tell you if a new project has been done before or if there are others who would like to work in a particular area. She can also suggest ways in which your assistance can be applied.

But, as the MCC of OGS president, she is in the best position to put you in contact with someone knowledgeable of the project. Computerize Inventory of the Research Room -- There is a need to prepare a computerized inventory of the contents of the MCC of OGS library or Research Room. Inventory a Cemetery -- One of the important long term projects that the MCC of OGS has underway is the location and inventory of all of the cemeteries in Monroe County. At present 220 private and public cemeteries have been identified. Perhaps 60% of these have been inventoried to date and about 30% have been published. Publication is proceeding and the rate of publication is currently controlled by the funds available. This task of inventorying cemeteries is an opportunity to get outdoors and enjoy the season while doing a very important job of preparing an inventory of the cemetery monuments. The task requires visiting the cemetery of your choice (coordinate with Karen Romick to avoid duplicating the work of someone else) with a notebook and pen and recording the names, dates and any other pertinent information from each monument. The name of the cemetery and good directions for reaching it is also needed. The resulting list needs to be eventually typed onto a computer by either the person making the inventory or at a later date by someone else. Eventually, the results will be published and credit given to the participants who implemented the task. There is considerable experience within the Chapter with the best ways of conducting this task, so contact Karen Romick to schedule a cemetery and to get any assistance that might be available.

Preserving Church Records in Monroe County Churches -- Church records have been one of the major sources of genealogical information over the centuries. The records of three German that spanned the period between 1831 and 1948 that were translated by Dr. Shirley Harmon and published in 2000 under the title, "Monroe County Church Records: Nineteenth Century Records of Lewisville, Middle (St. John s) and Miltonsburg (St. Peter s) German Evangelical Protestant Churches" are examples of the potential value of such records. The MCC of OGS has initiated a long term project to try to locate and preserve as many such records as possible. This is a project that is in need of a lead person to help develop a plan for the effort. It is also an opportunity for several volunteers who would work with the churches to locate and preserve records in a way that meets the needs of the church and the project. Volunteers may wish to limit their effort to only their own church or may choose to operate on a wider basis. This could be an unusually exciting project with many rewarding outcomes. Contact Karen Romick if this activity appeals to you at any level.

Monroe County Births, Deaths, Marriages, and Other Key Records -- One of the most arduous tasks in genealogy research is that of plowing through a maze of Probate Court and Health Department records in search of that specific ancestor or family line. It can be fun and exciting but it is labor intensive and frequently uncertain. A slight loss of concentration can cause an oversight of an important record or entry. Yet this process is repeated many times each year as new researchers trod the same path, looking through the same records and making the same mistakes as hundreds of others before them. One of the major impacts of the records being searched by so many people is the unfortunate damage and destruction to the documents themselves.

This is a task ideally suited for the computer. Once the records are placed into the computer memory, highly accurate searches can be done in a matter of a few seconds. Because of the ability of the computer to find accurately locate all entries, the search is much more conducive to discovering siblings, aunts, uncles, and all sorts of kin.

The MCC of OGS has undertaken several projects designed to computerize some of the most important Monroe County data to the genealogy community. These include records of birth, death and marriage in Monroe County, Ohio. About 50 years of marriage records have also been privately transcribed and published by Wes Cochran. Much more help in this area is needed.

This is one of those projects that can be worked on as time is available. Further, since most of these records are also available on microfilm, any volunteer with access to an LDS Family Research Centers can participate.

One of the interesting things about this task is that as the information is typed, the volunteer is treated to an impressive exposure to information that generates surprising insight about the people and places of the time. It is almost like being able to step back into time and witness many of the people and relationships of the day.

If you can spare a few hours a month (more or less) and would like to help with one of these important projects, please contact Karen Romick.

Computerize "Surname File" -- One of the big successes of the MCC of OGS has been the ever-growing surname file. Members and non-members can submit surnames for which they are doing research. People searching for common surnames may be able to exchange information of value to both searchers. In many cases the searchers are related and it becomes a useful way to discover relatives they never knew they had.

At present there are two somewhat limited ways to access these surname files. One is to come to the MCC of OGS Research Room and physically search through the files. The other is to happen to see the surname in an issue of the MCC of OGS newsletter, the Navigator.

One of the projects of the MCC of OGS is the computerize the Surname File so that it can be computer searched. This will not only make the information more widely available, it will allow for comparisons to be made among the surnames and searchers so that it will be easier to find potential relatives and sources of genealogical information.

Since the Surname File cards already have the information in written form, the primary task it to transfer the information to a computer disk. This is one of those tasks that can be divided among several people. If you have almost any kind of computer and could type data from the cards to a computer disk, please contact Karen Romick and arrange to help with this important project.

Typing Hand-written Information onto Computer Disks -- You will have noticed in the above projects that many may be originally recorded as hand-written information. For some projects such as the 'Cemetery Inventory" projects, it is almost essential that the original data be gathered as hand-written material. To convert these hand-written notes and data into publishable format it is essential that it be typed onto computer disks.

If you have a compute and a little time to devote, your help is urgently needed to help with the important task of converting material to computer media. Please contact Karen Romick and volunteer your support.

Look Ups -- One of the services that the MCC of OGS tries to provide is to respond to requests for information by people who are remotely located from the source of information or otherwise are not able to access it. As the expert source of Monroe County, Ohio genealogy, requests are frequently received that requires that someone be able to go to appropriate records for the answers. This labor-of-love is very rewarding in terms of friends made and the opportunities to help people otherwise unable to progress in their quest for their lineage. Queries vary widely from requests from information from records to looking for a grave in a specific cemetery.

While most of the look-up requests that the Chapter receives are likely to be for information in the Monroe County area, sometimes requests come in for information that is available only from places remote from Monroe County. An example might be a request for a look-up at a location in Missouri for information about a Monroe County family that moved to Missouri a century ago. The point is that you can be a look-up even though you may live in an area that is quite remote from Monroe County.

If you would be willing to be included on a list of possible 'Look Ups' please let Karen Romick know. The Look-up list is not published but is kept by the Chapter as a resource list. Being included on such a list does not commit you to perform look-ups that you may not wish to do.
