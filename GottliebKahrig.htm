CHRISTIAN GOTTLIEB KAHRIG

 

Christian Gottlieb Kahrig was born on December 12, 1796 in Rossnitz, Saxony, Germany and was baptized on December 13, 1796 in Leubnitz, Saxony. On October 9, 1839 he came to America. His bride to be, Elisabetha (Maria) Neuhart, arrived in America in 1839. She was born on April 15, 1809 in Rumbach, Palatinate, Germany.  Gottlieb and Maria were married on September 23, 1841 in Monroe County, Ohio and lived their entire life near Lewisville, Ohio. Mr. Kahrig was a carpenter and farmer. He and Maria attended the German Evangelical Church (Middle Church) near Lewisville, Monroe County, Ohio.

Five children were born to Gottlieb and Maria: Philipp 1842-1843; Carolina 1844-1873 married David Layer; Katharine 1845-1850; Ludwig (Louis) 1847-1911 married Catherine Gertler; and Anna Maria (Mary) 1850-1930 married Ludwig (Louis) Hehr.

On Friday the 20th of March 1868, Mr. Gottlieb Kahrig had gone to Woodsfield on business connected with the alteration of a road. After he had arranged his business, he at three o'clock started home. When about one fourth mile from town, he climbed over the fence into the field on the left of the road and had proceeded but a short distance when he fell dead. Mr. Lewis Hoeffler was returning home from his school in the country, discovered Mr. Kahrig lying in the field, he approached and upon examination, found that the life was extinct, he immediately notified several men who were at work in the fields adjacent. Information was brought to town, when Ed Salisbury Esq., summoned a jury and proceeded to hold an inquest upon the body.

The finding of the jury was, "That Gottlieb Kahrig, whose body was found within a field near the town of Woodsfield in Center Township, on the 20th day of March, 1868, came to his death by visitation of God in a natural way, and not by means of violence."

On June 18, 1887 Maria died of old age. Gottlieb, Maria and many of their descendants are buried in the St. John's Cemetery (Middle Church), near Lewisville, Ohio.

Gottlieb purchased his home and eighty acres from J.M. Stegner in 1866. Located in Summit Twp., Monroe County, Ohio, off St. Route 145, north of Lewisville, Ohio. Gottlieb and Maria's descendants continued to live in this home until 1979. The home is of log structure and remains standing as of this date in 1991.     Submitted hy Lena Kahrig Pettit
