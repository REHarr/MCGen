Biographical Sketch of Ferdinand Alexander and Ella Gusta (Stalder) Hedinger

 

Ferdinand Alexander and Ella Gusta (Stalder) HedingerSKETCH

 Source: Adams County History," copyright 1984, printed by Tylor Publishing Co, Dallas, Texas.  Submitted by Mr. and Mrs. Albert Hedinger.

 

        Ferdinand Alexander Hedinger, oldest child and son of Alexander and Mary Hugli Hedinger, was born July 6, 1889 on a small dairy farm near Sardis, Ohio, in Monroe County.  Another son died in infancy. 

       When Ferd was 9 years old, his father died.  He, his mother and sisters, Ida (Mrs.W.F. Walter) and Freda (Mrs. Tim Northup) took care of the farm for about five years.  The family moved from Ohio to a farm northwest of Nodaway, Iowa in Adams County, after Ferd's mother married Gottlieb Spring.  Ferd has a half brother, Raymond Spring.

       Ferd helped on the farm until he married Ellen Gusta  Stalder, December 12, 1912.  Ellen, the  youngest of eight children, was born to John and Mary Schell Stalder, March 29, 1890, on a farm three miles east of  Nodaway.  She had five brothers, Edward, who died at age 28, Fred, Bert, Oscar and James.  She also had two sisters, one who died in infancy when the family lived in Nebraska and Adaline who died in 1888 at age five.  Ferd's mother and Ella's father and all of Ferd's and Ella's grandparents were born in Switzerland.

        In the spring of 1913, Ferd and Ella moved to their farm four miles east of Nodaway.  They became the parents of three sons, Albert, Russell, and a son who died in infancy.  Ferd and Ella were active in community affairs.  Ella was a lifetime member of the Mt. Zion Presbyterian Church where she served as  primary Sunday School teacher for many years and was active in the Women's Society.  Ferd also  became a memer of the Mt. Zion Church and served in various church offices, being the Sunday School Superintendent for many years. Ferd worked with the Agricultural Stabilization and Conservation  Service (formerly the Agriculture Adjustment Administration) for many years at the County office in Corning, with bin construction and later caring for the grain and bin sites within the county.

       Ferd died February 11, 1963 and Ella died July 14, 1972.  They are at rest in Methodist Grove  Cemetery one and one-half miles from the farm home where they spent their married life.  They  celebrated their 50th Wedding Anniversary at the Mt. Zion Church in December 1962.
