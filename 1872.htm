Monroe County Obituaries

1872

 

Atkinson, Abel Atkinson

Published Spirit of Democracy March 5 1872

Abel Atkinson, son of Stephen Atkinson, Adams township, died on the 28th ult.  He served in the army three years during the late war.

 

Barnes. Little sister Barnes

Published Spirit of Democracy February 27 1872

Killed by Carelessness.  The people of Summerfield were shocked last Saturday morning with the information that Albert, son of Mr Nathan Barnes, living near that town had accidentally shot and killed his little sister: the boy aged about ten years, it appears had loaded a shot gun, and while handling it in a careless manner, it was discharged and the load of shot, striking his sister, a bright little girl, perhaps four years old, in the back of the head, produced instant death

 

Barnes, Little sister Barnes

Published Spirit of Democracy March 12 1872

In our account, week before last, of the melancholy accident by which the little daughter of Nathan Barnes lost her life, some errors were made.  The gun which was usually empty, had been that morning, leaded by an older brother, without the knowledge of any of the family; and had the little by been aware of the fact, he would not have touched it.  The family has been exceptionally careful in this regard, and no blame can attach to any one.

 

Brown, Joseph Brown

Published Spirit of Democracy March 13 1872

In our last issue we noticed the death of Mr. Joseph Brown.  We have since learned that he resided in Washington township, Belmont County.  His death was the result of an apoplectic stroke, and fall, which injured his skull.  Mr. Brown was 82 years of age and had been a resident of Belmont County for fifty years.

 

Brown, Joseph Brown

Published Spirit of Democracy March 5 1872

A man named Joseph Brown, residing near Beallsville, this county, went out to feed his stock the morning of the 25th ult., and not returning his wife made search for him; she discovered him near the barn with his skull crushed in.  He lived but a few hours, being unconscious to the time of his death.  Mr Brown was eighty years of age.

 

Caldwell, Rev. J B

Published Spirit of Democracy February 13, 1872

Rev. J. B. Caldwell of Uniontown, O. Died on Wednesday morning last week.  Mr. Caldwell for many years officiated as Presbyterian pastor in this place.  He was a large-hearted, good man and greatly beloved by all who enjoyed his friendship.

 

Castle, Mrs. Sarah M Castle

Published Spirit of Democracy January 30, 1872

Died - On the 25th inst., Mrs. Sarah M Castle, wife of T. O. Castle, of this place, after an illness of only four days.

       The angel messenger of death

       Has gently borne away

       A dear companion from our side

       To realms of endless day,

       Her voice no more will join with ours

       The song of praise below;

       It makes a purer, sweeter strain

       Where only pleasures flow

       When gathered on the Sabbath morn,

       Her vacant place we view

       Well think how bright the world she treads

       And in her steps pursue

       Be still, her very heart be still,

       And all our sorrow quell

       Well bow submissive to his Will

       Who doeth all things well.

 

Clark, Robt. Clark

Published Spirit of Democracy March 5 1872

Mr. Robt. Clark, an old citizen of this neighborhood, died a few days since near Mt. Pleasant, at an advanced age.

 

Curtis, S. J. Curtis

Published January 2 1872

Mr. S. J. Curtis of Carlisle this county, lost his life by a railroad accident at Monroeville, O. on Friday last.  He jumped or was knocked from the train while in motion, and was violently thrown to the ground, receiving injuries about the head which resulted in his death on Saturday.   Mr. Curtis leaves a family and many friends to mourn his loss.  The body was brought home for interment.  Noble County Republican 21st ult..

 

Dearth, Elizabeth Arn, nee Dearth
Source: Monroe County courthouse records, Woodsfield, Ohio, December 22, 1872
Elizabeth Arn, aged 77 years, 3 months and 18 days, born in Germany, died in Wayne township, Monroe County, Ohio, mother, Catherine Dearth

 

Dougherty, William Dougherty

Published Spirit of Democracy February 6, 1872

Died - At his residence in Washington township, Monroe County, Ohio, of Consumption on the 25th day of January 1872 William Dougherty in the 57th year of his age.

 

Eddy, Delia H Eddy

Published Spirit of Democracy January 23, 1872

On the 23d ult., of consumption , Delia H Eddy, consort of John Eddy, daughter of B. M. And Nancy Bolt, aged thirty three years, seven months and nine days.  W. O. B.

 

Fields, Albert Fields

Published January 2 1872

Boy Drown in the Muskingum

Marrietta, Dec 25 - a boy named Albert Fields, about seventeen years old, was drowned in the Muskingum River, at this place, nearly opposite the Congregational Church, on Sunday afternoon about 3 oclock, while the funeral services of three boys, drowned on Thursday, were in progress in the above church.  Fields was playing on the ice.  His body was recovered.

 

Hunt, James Hunt

Published Spirit of Democracy February 13, 1872

James Hunt of Spencers Station, died of apoplexy, last Wednesday evening.

 

Husselton, Susan Husselton

Forest, Mrs.

Published Spirit of Democracy February 13, 1872

Two more of St. Clairesvilles oldest citizens are gone.  Mrs. Susan Husselton died on Friday last, in the 89th year of her age.  Mrs. Forest died the same week in the 84th year of her age, at the residence of Mr. Robert Anderson.

 

Jackson, John W Jackson

Published Spirit of Democracy February 13, 1872

Died - On the 4th inst., in Clarington, this county, John W Jackson.

 

Lewis, Jesse Lewis

Published Spirit of Democracy March 5 1872

Died Suddenly - Mr. Jesse Lewis, a rather aged citizen of this place, died suddenly on Thursday morning of last week.  He had attended a meeting of the Sons of Temperance on Wednesday evening, where he appeared to be in his usual health and spirits.  As he was retiring for the night the attack came upon him, which ended in his death about one oclock in the morning.

 

Markley, Moses Markley

Published Spirit of Democracy 6 February 1872

Died - Of Paralysis in Washington township, this county, on Friday, the 26th day of January, 1872, at 10 oclock PM., Moses Markley, in  the 72nd year of his age.  Esquire Markley came to Monroe County from the northern part of this State, about the year 1840, and purchased a farm one mile west of the town of Graysville, in Washington township, on which he resided at the time of his decease.  Although he was not, properly speaking, a pioneer of the county, yet he came into it while he was still in the prime of life, and, by his indefatigable industry, contributed  much to the improvement of the country.  He was possessed of the most extraordinary physical strength and enjoyed almost constant good health up to a few weeks before his death.

His integrity and upright moral conduct secured the respect of who knew him, and he was not long a resident of the township before he was called to fill the office of Justice of the Peace, which office, he occupied, with great credit to himself for nine successive terms.  He was afterward elected Township Treasurer for several successive terms.  His manly worth and unimpeachable veracity made him a valued and esteemed citizen.  His honesty had long been proverbial in the neighborhood in which he lived, and in all the 32 years of which he was a resident of the county, there is not one act of his life that would not successfully bear the closest scrutiny of the most exact moralist.  Enemies, he had none.

In his religious views, Esquire Markley entertained the most unyielding faith in the final solution of all mankind, and no one ever entered more cheerfully on the journey to that undiscovered country, from whose bourne no traveler ever returns.

 

McManaway, Milton McManaway

Published Jan 2 1872

A rowdy Milton McManaway was shot and killed near Cambridge, the 23rd ult., by one of three men John Chapman, Tom Taylor or Albert Taylor.  Public sentiment is favorable toward the men who did the killing So says the Barnesville Enterprise.

 

McMannus, Mrs.

Published Spirit of Democracy February 27 1872

A woman named McMannus, living at Bellaire, Ohio, while walking on the platform at the blast furnace in Benwood, near Wheeling, on Monday, slipped on a loose board and fell to the ground, a distance of twenty five feet.  She was carrying her little boy in her arms at the time of the accident.  The woman was picked up for dead, but subsequently rallied a little.  Her spine was injured, and she died that evening.  The boy had a leg badly injured.

 

Mellott, child

Published Spirit of Democracy March 5 1872

Died  - On the 25th ult., child of Mr.  Mellotts of Jerusalem, this county.  Its death was sudden, the cause unknown

 

Miller, Mary Miller

Published Spirit of Democracy April 9 1872

Died - At the residence of Mr John Norris, Center township, on the 25th ult, Mary Miller, aged 80 years.

 

Mills, Wm B.

Published Spirit of Democracy March 5 1872

Sudden Death - Mr. Wm. B Mills, a well-known citizen of this place, died suddenly on Monday evening last.  He was found leaning over the bed, apparently trying to get on it, and quite insensible.  He was assisted into bed, and died in a few minutes.

 

Pickering, Mrs.  Pickering

Published Spirit of Democracy March 5 1872

Mrs Pickering, the oldest resident of Warren township, Belmont Coutny died in Barnesville week before last.

 

Plants, Mrs. Plants

Published Spirit of Democracy March 26 1872

The wife of Hon. T A Plants of this city, died in Cincinnat on Saturday last from the effect of a difficult operation performed on her several days before.  Her remains were brought home for burial, arriving on the Fleetwood on Monday morning.  Her funeral took place yesterday from the Methodist Episcopal Church, the services being conducted by Rev. John Goddard, of Cincinnati, of the new Jerusalem Church, in the doctrines of which the deceased was a believer.  She is spoken of by those who knew her intimately as a most estimable woman.  Mr Plants has the earnest sympathy of our entire community in his sad bereavement. - Meigs Co. Telegraph 13th inst.

 

Schmidt, Heinrich Schmidt
Heinrich Schmidt died May 30, 1872. He was born October 19, 1856, the son of Jacob and Maria Schmidt.

 

Schmidt, Philip Schmidt

Source: Spirit of Democracy, Woodsfield, Ohio, dated February 10, 1874

    Excerpt from "From Bethel Township" letter, Lebanon, O., Feb 3, 1874

    The funeral sermon of Phillip Schmidt was preached by Rev. Robins on the first of this month; after the sermon the following history of Mr. Schmidt was read:

    Philip Schmidt, born A. D. 1765, died January, 18, 1872, aged 107.  He became a member of the Lutheran Church at the age of 14, of which church he was a member for more than 70 years.  The last 10 or 12 years of his life has been spent as a consistent member of the M. E. Church.

 

Schneider, Elisabetha Schneider
Elisabetha Schneider died March 30, 1872. She was born in 1797, the daughter of George and Barbara Bott. In 1825, she married Friedrich Schneider.

 

Schwing, Mrs. Geo. Schwing

Published Spirit of Democracy March 5 1872

The wife of Mr. Geo. Schwing died very suddenly at her home in Cameron, this county, last week.  Mrs. Schwing was taken sick, the physician sent for, but before his arrival she had ceased to breathe.

 

Smittle, David Smittle

Published Spirit of Democracy January 16, 1872

Died on the 6th inst., David Smittle a resident of Lee township, this county.  We were pained to learn of the death of Mr. Smittle; he was a good citizen and one of the best men socially and morally twas ever our fortune to become acquainted with.  The citizens of Lee will mourn him as one of her most well loved citizens.  Peace to his remains.

 

West, Wallace West

Published Spirit of Democracy April 2 1872

Died of Pulmonary Consumption in Bethel township, Wallace West on the 20th March, 1872, aged 24 years.

He left a good impression upon the minds of his friends of his acceptance with God in retaining his mental faculties to the last moment of his life, and was heard to say, I am happy and gave a farewell to those around  him, as though he were going on a peaceful journey - Thus another good man has gone before     K

 

Wiegan, Dr.  Wiegan

Published Spirit of Democracy March 19 1872

The body of Dr. Wiegan, of Zanesville, Ohio, was found in the Licking River near that place on the 4th inst.  Dr Wiegan disappeared from his home on the 18th day of January.  The coroners jury returned a verdict of suicide by drowning.

 

Wiley, John Wiley

Published Spirit of Democracy February 13, 1872

Mr. John Wiley an aged citizen of Sharon  township, and a soldier of the War of 1812, was prostrated by paralysis on Sunday evening last.  The stroke was so severe as to cause his death on last Tuesday evening.
