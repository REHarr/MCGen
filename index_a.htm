**NOTICE**
The Monroe County Chapter of the Ohio Genealogical Society has announced a
limited reprint of its 1992 publication, "Monroe County, Ohio Families."
This volume is the only history of its type ever done on Monroe County.
Family researchers submitted over 900 articles filled with family stories
and rare photos that tell the unique history of Monroe County.  The 351
page book is a beautiful 9 x 12 hardbound volume with an index.  In a
special offer, the chapter will be selling the book at its 1992 price of
$55.00. (Postage is $4.00 per book and Ohio residents add $3.58 tax.)   To
guarantee a copy in this limited reprint, the chapter is now accepting
prepaid advance sales.  To reserve your copy, send your order to the
Monroe Chapter OGS, Box 641, Woodsfield, OH  43793.  For questions, email:
neiswong@1st.net or call Karen Romick at 740-483-1481.

PURPOSE:
 
The Monroe County Chapter of The Ohio Genealogical Society was 
chartered in 1984 as a chapter of the Ohio Genealogical Society. Our 
purpose is to promote genealogy and preserve genealogical data in our 
county. We share this inforation with others and may provide the link 
you are seeking.

RESEARCH ROOM: Visit the Research Room in the Monroe County District Library, 96 Home Avenue, Woodsfield, 43793 (740-472-1954). Our chapter maintains this collection of data on Monroe County and related areas. Donations are welcome. Summer Hours: Mon 10:00 - 8:00; Tues-Sat 10:00 - 5:00 Sept thru May: Mon-Fri 10:00 - 8:00; Sat 10:00 - 5:00 SURNAME FILE: The Surname File is located in the Research Room at the Monroe County District Library. Surnames are also published in the newsletter. Members should submit their Monroe County surnames on 3 x 5 cards (one name per card). Each card should include the surname being researched and your name and address. Send your cards to the Monroe Chapter, OGS. FIRST FAMILIES and PIONEER FAMILIES OF MONROE COUNTY: First Families Of Monroe County: This is a department of the Monroe Chapter, OGS. It is open to any member who is a direct descendent of an individual living in Monroe County by 31 December 1830. An application detailing proof of descent must be submitted with a one-time $15 application fee. For more information write to us! The deadline for applications for the joining First Families in 2000 is August 31. The banquet is $6.50 per plate and the reservations are due September 9, 2000. For a printable request for this application form click here. Pioneer Families Of Monroe County This is a new department of the Monroe Chapter, OGS. This department does not replace First Families but works in conjunction with it. To join Pioneer Families, you must submit an application detailing proof of descent. Include documents providing proof plus a one-time $15.00 application fee. If your family is already enrolled in First Families, then the application fee for Pioneer Families is only $10.00 It is open to any member who is a descendent of an individual who settled in Monroe County by 31 December 1850. The deadline for applications for the joining Pioneer Families in 2000 is August 31. The banquet is $6.50 per plate and the reservations are due September 9, 2000.
