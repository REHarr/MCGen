Notes for ANNA M. MEEK:

  Mrs. Columbia Harsh, wife of William H. Harsh, of Caldwell, has received word of the death of her sister,  Mrs. Anna Meek Carpenter, aged seventy years, widow of J. A. Carpenter, which occurred at her home in Springfield, Ohio, last Wednesday.  Her husband died eighteen years ago and since his death she had resided at Springfield.

  She was the daughter of the late Mr. and Mrs. N. M. Meek, of Summerfield, and was born at Antioch, Monroe County.  She was a member of the Church of God, at Springfield.

  Seven children survive with one brother, Charles H. Meek, of Summerfield, and two sisters, Mrs. Harsh, of Caldwell and Mrs. Mary Keylor, of Woodsfield.  One brother, J. W. Meek is deceased.

  Funeral services were held Saturday in the Church of God and burial was made in a cemetery at Huntsville.  Thursday 2 July 1942 Noble County Leader.

 

 

  Mrs. Anna Carpenter, 69, died at 1:45 P.M. Wednesday in the home of her daughter, Mrs. David Duer, National Road west, Springfield Route 1. She had been in failing health for the past four years years and had been seriously ill for three months.

  A native of Summerfield, Mrs. Carpenter was the daughter of Newton and Suzanna Meek.  She had resided in Springfield for the past 10 years, and was the widow of John A. Carpenter, who died in 1926 in Huntsville.

  Funeral services will be held at 2 P.M. Saturday in the Second Church of God, of which she was a member. Burial will be in Huntsville Cemetery, Huntsville. The body was removed to the Richards Funeral Home, and was returned to the home of the daughter Thursday afternoon.

  Surviving are four daughters, Mrs. Duer, in whose home she died, Mrs. J. C. Keeren, Urbana; Mrs. Clyde Fair and Mrs. Robert Yoder, both of Springfield; three sons, Frank of Russells Point, and Joseph and Ray, both Springfield; two sisters, Mrs. Will Harsh, Caldwell, O., and Mrs. Frank Kaylor, Woodsfield, and one brother, Charles Meek of Summerfield.  25 June 1942 Springfield News Springfield Ohio.
