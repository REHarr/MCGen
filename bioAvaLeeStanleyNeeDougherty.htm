Biographical Sketch of Ava Lee Stanley nee Dougherty

 

Ava Lee Stanley nee Dougherty

Source:  MONROE COUNTY DOUGHERTY CLAN, by James McKitrick, 1989, p 174

          Ava Lee Dougherty was born at 2:05 pm, on 19 August 1933, in Parkersburg, West Virginia.  She attended Jefferson school and graduated from Parkersburg High school in 1952.  On 31 July 1952 she married Ray M. Stanley.  Ava worked at several jobs but was mainly a wife, mother and homemaker.  Ray was born in Parkersburg and attended Jefferson Elementary School.  Ray, like  Ava, graduated from Parkersburg High School.  Ray worked at Dils Brothers Department Store until his enlistment in the US Air Force.  Ray then spent 20 years with theAir Force being assigned in such places as Ashuija AFB, Japan, 1952:  Berstrom AFB, Texas, 1954; Matagora Island Bombing and Gunnery Range 1957, Lake Charles AFB, Louisiana, 1958; Cardswell AFB, Texas, 1958; and Elemendorf AFB, Alaska, 1959.  Ray and Ava were in Alaska when the great Alaskan earthquake hit.  He was assigned to Steubenville, Ohio in 1964 for recruiter duty.  His other assignments are Lackland AFB, Texas, 1967.  He retired in 1972 going to work for civil service.  He returned to school and earned his BA in 1978 from SW Texas State University.  His major is Occupational Education.  In 1980 he received his Masters from SWTSU in Interdisciplanary [sic] Sciences.  He is currently working as Program Administrator for Technical Training for the USAF at Randolph AFB, Texas.  This family has contributed many items for the various auctions at the Dougherty-Daugherty reunions.  They also wrote the Dougherty-Daugherty O'COOKBOOK, put out in 1984.  As Ray says about Ava Lee, she has received her degree in Hard-Knocks and common

sense traumas and triumphs from the house of Stanley and Kits.
