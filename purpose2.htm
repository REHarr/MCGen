PURPOSE B WHY MCC of OGS EXISTS

AND WHY YOU SHOULD BE A MEMBER

 

Of the many hundreds of thousand of life-forms that inhabit this world of ours that we call AEarth,@ it appears that man is the only one to possess an ability to accumulate knowledge, learn from it, and to build upon it. While this process of accumulating knowledge takes many forms, we generally refer to the process by which it occurs as history. One focus of this history is genealogy. Webster defines genealogy as being a recorded history of one=s ancestry; the study of family descent.

Man has done a fairly good job recording his history and accumulating knowledge. Ironically, however, people seldom record for posterity the events of their own lives. It usually remains for the genealogist and historian to sort through records, newspapers, memories, and whatever information they can find to retrospectively create the recorded history of one=s ancestry. This process is viewed in many ways by genealogists who have alternately described it as being interesting, laborious, captivating, time consuming, rewarding, tedious, addictive and many other adjectives. But, the best genealogists among us are never quite able to capture the personalities of their subjects. Only faceless facts are found among the trails that are left behind which are often in hidden resources tucked away in musty courthouses, in newspaper morgues and various other places.

Undaunted by the difficulties they encounter the genealogist, who is frequently just a curious kin interested in discovering a little more about themselves and their family, must press ahead. Most frequently they will read guidance that has been placed in the literature by more experienced researchers to try to discover the optimum methods for searching. They will go to the record books that have been opened thousands of times by an equal number of researchers pursuing their own ancestry. They will rediscover for the thousandth time the records that exist; the fact that certain records were lost to fire; the facts that their family line may have been previously researched; and that certain brick walls exist that seem to bring their search to a dead end. They may make the same mistakes of data interpretation or discover work-arounds and leads that may have been previously discovered by others. WouldnÂ’t it be great if a resource were available to help the new and experienced genealogist find their way through the genealogical thicket in an efficient way?

Well, there is such a resource. One of the ways that the genealogy community has found to improve their efficiency, limit their work, share their processes and methodologies, and share their experiences is to form organizations to help focus their efforts. The Monroe County Chapter of the Ohio Genealogical Society (MCC of OGS) is one such group serving the Monroe County Ohio area.

The purpose of the MCC of OGS is to promote genealogy and preserve genealogical data in our County. We share information with others that may be the link to your family roots. MCC of OGS welcomes all-comers whether members or visitors. We maintain several resources that are available to members and visitors that include a well stocked library, a free web site, and courteous, friendly officers and staff to help you. MCC of OGS can help to provide a focus for your research through these and other resources and through help provided by Chapter members and our friendly officers and staff. Certain additional benefits are available to members such as the award winning newsletter that is published quarterly, the opportunity to participate in the First Family and the Pioneer Family programs, the ability to place your queries in the newsletter at no cost, and more. The additional benefits of being a member are certainly well worth the small annual membership fee of $10 per year.
