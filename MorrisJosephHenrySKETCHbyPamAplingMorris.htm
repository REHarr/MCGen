JOSEPH  HENRY  MORRIS

At the request of the DAR (Daughters of the American Revolution), this sketch was written in 2017 by Pamela (Apling) Morris, wife of Thomas Joseph Morris, for publication in the First Families of Perry County, Indiana, book.

 

       Joseph Henry Morris was the first child born to David C. and Margaret A. Fulkerson Morris.  He was born in Monroe Co., Ohio; ten months after the couple were married.  It is said that David moved his family to Ranger, IN in 1865 due to the Civil War.  However it was not before Joseph Henry enlisted.  On the 18th of March 1862, the day after his 18th birthday, he joined the OH 7th Light Artillery Battery.  He served for a period of three years, as rank of Private.

       After the War Joseph joined his family in Perry County, IN, he married Hannah E. Windlan and seven children were born to them.  (It is thought that Hannah either came with the family and waited for Joseph with them in Perry County or that she waited for him in Ohio with her family and traveled to Perry County with Joseph.  Since the marriage took place in Perry County it might suggest that she came ahead with the Morris family.)

       On the 29th day of July 1886, Joseph bought, from John Harland for the sum of Three Hundred and Fifty Dollars, Forty acres to the South of Ranger.  Hannah died the next year and two years after their last child was born, leaving Joseph alone to raise the children.  He did so for three years.  He then married, on the 20th of October 1890, Melvina Hayes Cooper. She was the widow of Francis Marion Cooper of Spencer County, IN.  She too was raising young children; Charles Wesley, Eva Lena and Florence Leona Cooper.  The couple started a family of their own adding three more children; Christopher L., Kathryn P. and George W. Morris.  Those children would later move and start families in IL.  Their son, Christopher L. Morris was a Corporal in the US Army.  Christophers nephew, Wayne, Georges son, was in the Army Air Force during WWII. 

       In talking to the descendants of Joseph Henry Morris, he seems to be a man of some mystery, as no known photo has very been found, however a man of hard work and great carpentry skill.  To his credit he helped dig two prominent lakes; Fenn Haven Lake in Tell City and Lincoln Lake in Spencer County.  It is here that he is said to have become ill, never fully recovering and leading to his death.

       Another Perry County landmark that is credited to him is the big red barn along old highway 37 near Gatchel.  The barn was built around 1894 and a Henry Morris is named when asked who built it.  The barn is built in the same manner as the barns of Monroe County, Ohio and is a massive structure for the Perry County area.  Josephs father also built barns in Ohio and in the Ranger area in Perry County.

       The family has been informed by the Lincoln Boyhood National Memorial Park officials that Joseph was one of the last to be buried in the parks cemetery before being closed to burials, due to the Presidents mother being buried there.  Morris family members that are also buried in the cemetery are; Susanna Morris, Luann Morris and William T. Morris Jr.  Only Joseph and his sister in law, Susanna, wife of Thomas, are marked.   It was assumed for some time that Thomas, Josephs brother, was also buried there.  However, family members have since found his grave in nearby Richardson Cemetery, Lincoln City, Spencer County.
