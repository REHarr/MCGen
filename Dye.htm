          The Dye family tree is one of the larger family trees on the Monroe County, Ohio History and Genealogy websites.  As a single family tree it includes 16 generations and numbers about 22,440 entries.  Because of its size it is difficult to present the entire family tree as a single file on the website.

     A large part of this Dye family tree, provided by Alan Berry, is made up of the lineage of three 5th generation ancestors.  The three ancestors are sons of the 4th generation ancestor, John Laurens Dye  born 1687 Staten Island, Richmond Co., NY.  These three 5th generation Dye ancestors are: John Lawrence Dye  born 1711, James Dye  born 1720, and Vincent Dye born 20 Jul 1726.  All three sons were born in Staten Island, Richmond Co., NY.  (John Laurens Dye  b born 1687 had 8 children.  The above three sons are presented in the greatest detail in Alan Berry’s Dye tree.)  

     To make the presentation of the Dye family tree more manageable, it has been divided into 2-parts.  Each part begins with the same ancestor, Dytszen [Derrick] DUYTS  born about 1589 Denmark, and includes the subsequent 4-generations.  That is, each of the 2-parts are identical through the first 5 generations.  Beginning with the 5th generation, however, one part of the tree (Dye-1a) follows/includes John Lawrence Dye born 1711 and James Dye born 1720.  Vincent Dye born 20 Jul 1726 has been excluded from Dye-1a to reduce the file size.  Beginning with the 5th generation the other part of the tree (Dye-1b) follows/includes Vincent Dye born 20 Jul 1726 and excludes John Lawrence Dye born 1711 and James Dye born 1720 to reduce the file size.  The detailed lineage of the excluded brothers can been seen by consulting their respective trees.

     The identification of the two parts in the Dye family tree in the Family Trees index is as follows:

                             Dye-1a - John Lawrence Dye born 1711 and James Dye born 1720    

                             Dye-1b - Vincent Dye born 20 Jul 1726

     For completeness, all of the siblings from the 5th generation of all of the branches are given in each tree.  Also, the complete available lineages of all other branches are included on each part of the trees for completeness and clarity for the reader.
