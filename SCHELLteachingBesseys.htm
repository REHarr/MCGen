The Teaching Besseys—16 Strong

 

“Dib’s Diary” column in The Fresno Bee—about 1961*

By Redford H. Dibble (MIIL)

 

     Did somebody say something about dedicated teachers?

     Well, here we have for sure a Tulare County record and for all we know a United States record.

     There are three Bessey men who are superintendents of schools.  Their wives all teach.  There are other brothers who used to teach and whose wives teach or did teach.  There are daughters and sons-in-law of the Bessey boys who teach or are getting ready to teach.

     Altogether there are 16 of them in the profession.

     Before we get all tangled up and make an error in pedagogical protocol, we’d better start at the beginning.

     It all began with Mr. and Mrs. Merritt Bessey, who were cattle ranchers in the Poplar area, arriving there about 1917.  She, Mrs. Lura Bessey, now lives in Porterville and in her younger days taught in Rooks County, Kan.

     So it appears that love for teaching is hereditary.

     Mrs. Bessey’s three sons who are superintendents are:

     Ray L. Bessey, Tipton.  He lives in Tulare and his wife, Verna, teaches at Oak Valley.

     Jay L. Bessey, Farmersville, whose wife, Helen, teaches in Porterville.

     Ronald S. Bessey, Richgrove, whose wife, Myrtice, teaches in Terra Bella.

     Another brother, Roscoe Bessey, lives in Corcoran.  He formerly taught and coached.  His wife, Beatrice, teaches there now.

     Still another, Corliss Bessey, is an automobile dealer in Patterson and taught for a short time years ago.  But---surprise!—his wife didn’t and doesn’t.

     And still another, Gerald Bessey, Corcoran, is a gasoline dealer.  Neither he nor his wife teaches.

     Then, on the feminine side of the family, there is a sister, the former Elaine Bessey, who teaches in the Pasadena area.  And her husband, Olaf Frodsham, is a music instructor at Occidental College.

     There are two other sisters and their husbands in the family, but they “got away”, too.  None teaches.  They are Mr. and Mrs. Richard (Dorine) Landers, Ventura, and Mr. and Mrs. Richard (Nadine) Hardaway, Merced.

     So for that generation.

     Mr. and Mrs. Ronald  Bessey have a daughter Janet, who is now Mrs. Ken Shannon and lives in the Los Angeles area.  Oh, sure.  They both teach.

     They have another daughter, Eleanor, now Mrs. James Graham, also in the Los Angeles area.  He teaches and she is going to college.  Her major?  Education, of course.

     Mr. and Mrs. Corliss Bessey have a daughter who is right in the groove.  She’s Mrs. Judy Pickup and she teaches in the Fresno City Schools.

     The meeting of the BEA (Bessey Education Association) will please come to order.
