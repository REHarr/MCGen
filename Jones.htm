1. Ephraim JONES was born on 12 Aug 1751 in Woodbridge, Middlesex, New Jersey.1 Between 1778 and 1782 he was a shoemaker in Muncy Township, Northumberland, Pennsylvania.2 He served in the military Revolutionary War in 1779 in Pennsylvania.2 They arrived in Monroe County in 1796.[i][1] Ephraim died after 1832 at the age of 81 probably in Monroe County, Ohio.

 

Information on the service of Ephraim Jones can be found at the website of Sandra Jones Hall, including affidavits by Charles Atkinson, Phoebe & John Jones:

http://freepages.genealogy.rootsweb.ancestry.com/~sandrajoneshall/Rev-Petition.htm


Phoebe HARNED was born between 1760 and 1770. She died after 20 Dec 1832 at the age of 72 in probably Monroe County, Ohio. Ephraim Jones married Phoebe Hornet/Harned 19 Jul 1773 in Middlesex Co. NJ. They appeared in the census in 1790 in Northumberland County, Pennsylvania.

[FROM NOTES OF B. F. ATKINSON ON THE JONES FAMILY. HE WAS THE GRANDSON OF MARGARET (JONES) ATKINSON.]
     "The Jones family emigrated from England and were of Welsh blood. They came at an early date, prior to the Revolutionary War to the colonies. The original family of brothers were divided in the war sentiment; out of the three or four brothers, only one was true to the Colonial Army and this one was the father of Judge John Jones. One brother resided in Baltimore and returned to England, leaving a large amount of real estate which was confiscated by the U.S.A.

     "John and another brother moved from New Jersey; the brother procured a farm on Cranes' Nest Creek near Newcastle in Monroe Co. OH. He erected a mill on the stream. He was the father of a large family. The boys were Abraham, Asher, Hamilton, and Lewis. There were at least three girls.

     "John Jones settled on Bull Creek, Virginia, (now in Wood County, WV) where about 1794, he married Lillie Benjamin. He moved to the Scioto Valley about 1802 and thence to a farm north of Newark, OH, where his wife, Lillie died in 1802. He with James Atkinson moved their families from near Newark, OH to the Ohio Valley where he bought a farm on Proctor Creek in Virginia (now WV) opposite Clarington, Monroe Co. OH where Sunfish Creek empties into the Ohio River. He then removed and settled on Sunfish Creek at Jonesville, below Cameron, Monroe Co.; here he erected a mill on the farm, which operated as a saw and grist mill."

1A. James JONES was born on 14 Oct 1800 in Ohio Township, Monroe County, Ohio.11 He died on 28 Aug 1870 at the age of 69 in Monroe County, Ohio. James was buried in Jones Family Cemetery, Green Township, Monroe, Ohio. This is referred to as the Grassy Knoll Cemetery in Catharine's obituary. The family Bible identifies him as a son of Ephraim and Phoebe Jones.

 

Catharine DRIPS was born on 19 Nov 1805 in Waynesburg, Greene, Pennsylvania. Monroe County Death record lists age at death as 82y 7m 9d which would make her birthday Jan 1806 instead. Catharine was buried in Jones Family Cemetery, Green Township, Monroe, Ohio.  The Drips name is found in Greene County, PA, where Catharine was born.  Some of the family of Thomas Drips of Ireland, settled in southwestern Pennsylvania.  In the 1880 census, Catharine gave her father's birthplace as Ireland.
