Album of Photos and Graphics

for Wolfe and Related Family Lines

 

Family Tree Maker, Version 2014 that has been used in the preparation of this book has only limited capabilities for embedding graphics into the contents of a genealogy book which would be the preferred method of presenting photos and other graphics. Therefore, the graphics have been incorporated into the following album. Each graphic (most are photographs) is cross referenced to a location in the text of the book where that graphic would appear if it were embedded.

 

Family crests appear throughout this book. Family crests or coats-of-arms were important symbols used on flags, banners, shields, stationery, etc. in the early days of Europe to identify a family line. They are added here primarily for their decorative value. As is true today, many unrelated families shared a common name such as Wolfe. Each Wolfe family may have their own crest or coat-of-arms. When members of these families immigrated to America, the use of crests were largely discontinued and usually forgotten. As a result, we do not know which of the several crests associated to a given surname, might actually apply to our families that bear that name – if any. So while the crests used in this book are actually crests of the indicated name, they may or may not be the appropriate ones for the families in this book.
