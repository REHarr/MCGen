Phillip, the sixth child of Ludwig (Louis) and Catherine Gertler Kahrig, was born May 6, 1880 in the home of the Kahrig Homestead, which his grandpa Gottlieb Kahrig purchased in 1866 located 2 miles north of Lewisville on State Rt. 145. Phillip was baptized on May 23, 1880 and in 1894 was confirmed at St. John's Evangelical Church (Middle Church) where he attended church his entire life. He walked to and attended Owl Hollow School which was located just north of the home on State Route 145.

Phillip had three sisters and two brothers older than he: Caroline, (1870-1938), married Philip Rhines, (1867-1934); Henry, (1871-1934), married Carrie Kirsch, (1863-1939); Ludwig (Lewis), (1876-1876); Anna Maria (Mary), (1878-1962), married Gustav Kilburn, (1873-1955). He also had four brothers and one sister younger than he: Frederick, (1881-1968), married Teresa Burkhart, (1888-1979); brother born and died 1883; John Benjamin, born and died, 1885; Charles (Carl), (1888-1897); Freda (1892-1960), married (l) Joseph Weir, (2) Mr. Bolt. In 1904, Phillip was united in marriage to Louisa Lucetta Pfalzgraf, born 1882, the daughter of George and Sophia Kilbum Pfalzgraf. They were married in St. Peter's Church, Miltonsburg, Ohio and began housekeeping at the Kahrig Homestead. In 1906, Louis and Catherine sold the farm and home to Phillip and Louisa. (Louis and Catherine purchased a home on Holiday Avenue in Woodsfield, Ohio.)

In 1916, lightening struck the original barn and a new one was built on the same location. Horses were used for farming. Other livestock consisted of lambs, cattle, and chickens. Hay and grain was grown for feeding the livestock. Oil lamps were used in the home and wood and coal stoves were used for heating and cooking. In 1925, carbide lights were installed in the home. Electric lights were installed in 1948.

Louisa and Phillip's ten children were born in the home: Lawrence, born 1905, married Ella Christman, born 1906, (14 children); Alfred born 1907, married Gertrude Christman, born 1911, (8 children); George, born (1908-1990), married Laura Christman, born 1913 (7 children); Edwin, (1910-1988), married Mary Truax, (1913-1979), (2 children); Marie, born 1914, m 1937, div. 1967, Hugh Brooks, (1912-1976) (7 children); Benjamin, born 1916, married (I) Nellie Tillet, (4 children), (2) Patricia Ingram, born 1922; Homer, born 1918, married Velva Eagon, born 1920; Sophia, born 1912, married Clemence Reischman, (1913-1990), (2 children); and Minnie, born 1923, m 1948, div. 1979, Robert Waiters, born 1926 (2 children).

In 1960, Phillip and Louisa sold their last car, a 1932 Chevrolet. After 62 years of marriage, Phillip died suddenly in 1967, at his home. Louisa continued to stay at the home until Summit Acres Nursing Home was a necessity. She died in 1977. Louisa and Phillip are buried in Middle Church Cemetery.

Louisa's hobby was piecing quilts and Phillip's was splitting oak wood to make baskets for variable uses. He also teamed in oil fields. Many of the descendants are still residing in Monroe County.             Submitted by Alfred M. Kahrig
