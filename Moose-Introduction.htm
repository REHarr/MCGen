The Families of Clifford I. and

Martha Ellen Hall Moose

by

Carl D. Weber

                          

Introduction

 

This Family History is based mainly on the results obtained by utilizing on-line resources at Ancestry.com, the records of Monroe County, Ohio and personal information. Also, several cousins have provided key information. During the time of the collection of the basic information for my parent’s family history, I discovered that I could extract enough information to begin to construct a more detailed history of my Grandparents, Clifford and Mattie Hall Moose. Apparently, no one has published a History or Family Tree on Martha Ellen (Mattie) Hall’s Family since my initial searching found no connection to any on-line family trees or other sources. However, after establishing several family connections, I was able to find several family trees on-line that contained considerable information on some branches of Mattie Hall’s Family. I also discovered “new cousins” who provided valuable leads and family information. Research in Monroe County is difficult because of the fire that burned vital records in 1867. However, the excellent work by the Monroe County Genealogical Society with the books and CDs that they offer has certainly been of prime importance to me in writing this history. This history was written initially for my own family.

 

My mother was Helen Mayme Moose, the 7th child of Clifford and Martha Moose. She married Carl Willard Weber of Summit Township in Monroe County in 1934. My older brother Jack and I were born in Jacob Weber’s house near Lewisville. Around Thanksgiving Day in 1936, my father was accidentally shot in the right wrist in a hunting accident and as a result he had a difficult time obtaining meaningful employment. This was a significant event for our family as there were no “hire the handicap laws” at that time. So we moved around a lot in the oilfields as my Father tried to find descent jobs.

 

In 1938 or 1939, we moved to Gladwin, Michigan where my father had obtained a job in the oilfields most likely through the help of his brother-in-law, Will Lafferee. After Will and Dad’s sister Lilly moved away to Deerfield, Michigan in 1940, our family grew up somewhat isolated from all other family members with the exceptions of two cousins and my uncle and aunt.  I seldom saw either of the two grandparents who were alive when I was born. As a result I knew very little about my ancestors, aunts, uncles and cousins. So this is one of the reasons for this endeavor as I tried to find out all I could about my parent’s families.

 

The basis for a portion of this history was found in the Family Trees on Ancestry.com’s, One World Tree. When possible, this information has been verified by other sources such as census listings and Family Collection Data, County Records, Death Records, reading grave markers and through books obtained from the Monroe County Branch of the Ohio Genealogical Society.  However, not all of the information included in this book has been fully substantiated. On Ancestry.com, I found Family Trees1,2,3 that traced the history of the Moose Family. Also found was information on the Moose Family in “Monroe County Ohio Families” published by the Monroe County Genealogical Society4. An article on the Moose Family submitted by Dick Moose5 provided starter information and Dick also provided other documents on the Moose Family of Monroe County.

 

Some of the additional names associated with the Moose Family are Moore, Baker, Nalley, Shafer, Hess, Vanwy, Armbuster, Hoffman, Earley, Fuger, Rouse, Bates, Piatt, Weber, Doan, Orange Mobberly, Roach, Winland and Repple.

 

Key names in the Hall Family are Hall, Hamilton, Ullom, Mitchell, Wood, Bowen, Bashore, Wenrich, Mauer, Moon, Humphrey, Wilsford, Burgess, Dilworth, Hill, Wood, Osborne and Teague. No on-line family tree could be found for the Hall Family.

 

In this History, I will attempt a detailed study of the ancestors and descendants of the Clifford Moose and Mattie Hall Families in Monroe County and beyond. I will also include as many photographs as I can obtain.

 

I would like to give special thanks to Marlene and Dick Moose, Marge Billman, Chris Creamer, Shelby and John Perry, Carrie Bates, Ralph Christman and Linda Grible for their valuable contributions in helping me track the Moose Family and for providing several photographs. In particular, Connie Koniski, who provided information, guidance and photos and a GEDCOM file of her work on the Moose Family has been very helpful in forming this family history.  .

 

There may be some inconsistencies in structure in this history but I hope that will be offset by the content.

 

I selected the following outline for presenting the information:

 

1. Generations of a particular family are listed as follows:

 

First generation in a line selected for this history is listed as 1.

Second        2

Third           3

Fourth         4

Fifth            5

Sixth            6

Etc.

 

2. The lineage of each person covered here, will be listed with his name. Also, the birth and death date of each person will be listed when describing his family.

 

3. Abbreviations:   Abt = About

                             Bet= Between

                             Bef= Before

                             b.= born

                             d.= died

 

4. Since it is repeated so often in this history, Monroe in any listing refers to Monroe County, Ohio.  For instance, an entry of “Summit, Monroe, Ohio” means  Summit Township, Monroe County, Ohio. Any Monroe entry that is not Monroe County, Ohio will be designated. The same is true for Belmont, Jefferson and Tuscarawas Counties.
