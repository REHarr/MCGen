This “Programme” of the 1905 Belmont-Monroe “Reunion” will soon be 100 years old.  So, it is understandable that it is a little faded.  The text of the program reads as follows:

 

 

The Belmont-Monroe

RE-UNION!

 

To be held in Griffith’s Grove,

-        Jerusalem, Ohio  -

 

Tuesday, August 29, 1905

 

 

Programme.

 

 

9:30  A.M.      Prayer by …………………………………… Rev. Harry Scott, Barnesville

Music by the Barnesville City Band

Address or Welcome …………………………………………… Dr. S. D. Griffith, President

10:00 A.M.     Address by ……………………………………… Rev. Austin Wilson, Clarington

10:30    “         Duet “Yuba Dam,”  Misses Lulu and May Crum, Beallsville

10:30    “         Address --- Subject, Barnesville …………………… Mayor, J. A. White

11:30 A. M.    Dinner.

12:30 P.M.     Concert by …………………………………………… Barnesville City Band

  1:00    “         OLD SOLDIERS’ HOUR --- Campfire, Singing, “Grand Army Bean”

                        by a number of old soldiers.  Miss Emma Eaton will also assist

with a number of selections on the Graphophone --- ex-

cerpts of orations from Lincoln, Mckinley and others.

  2:00 P.M.     Address --- Monroe County ………………… Mansel Gallagher, Woodsfield

  2:30 P.M.     Address --- Belmont County ………………… G. A. Colpitts, Barnesville

Music --- Barnesville City Band

  3:00 P.M.     Address by ………………………… Judge  W. F. Smith, Barnesville

  3:30 P.M.     RECITATION CONTEST --- Recitation --- “What the Train Brought,”

                        by Miss Rosa Baker, Beallsville.  “Red Jacket,” by Miss

                        Anna Hayes, Beallsville.  Also, Recitations by two ladies

from Beallsville.

  4:00 P.M.     OLD FIDDLERS’ CONTEST --- A number have expressed a desire

                        to enter this contest.  The list is still open.

Þ       Hon. John M. Amos, of Cambridge, will also be present and give us

one of his interesting talks.

        Committee recall the “The Tin Wedding” feature --- too many applications.

        Executive Session of Committee at noon hour.  New drilled well and

water tank.  Feed furnished for horses on the ground.

By order of             COMMITTEE

 

 

Provided by Shirley Neiswonger
