STORIES OF MONROE COUNTY

Monroe County, Ohio is truly one of the jewels among the counties of our nation. As one of the frontier counties as the nation expanded westward, Monroe County was witness to fierce struggle between the Native Americans and encroaching settlers. Stories abound from this period that would challenge our best movie industry for action, heroism, defeat, courage and progress. The County saw an oil boom that changed the culture of its people forever. In this section several of the important stories, events and informational sources that helped define the history and genealogy of Monroe County are included. They help set the stage for the periods in Monroe County history in which our ancestors lived. Some are just entertaining.

Click here for a list of the available stories of Monroe County

 

TIME-LINE OF MONROE COUNTY

Throughout its history Monroe County, Ohio experienced many people and events that are noteworthy in understanding the lives of our ancestors and the people today. A time-line beginning with factors that eventually led to the formation of the County and subsequently shaped the County is being constructed.  This is a work in progress that will be opened to the website in the near future.

 

LATITUDE AND LONGITUDE OF SELECTED MONROE COUNTY SITES

Work is in progress to locate specific sites in Monroe County to within 20 feet using their latitude and longitude.  Global Positioning System (GPS) data are available for certain selected sites such as towns, churches, cemeteries, grave-sites, stores, houses, and more.  Learn how the Global Positioning System and GPS units work.  Find out how you can add data to the GPS database.  This database is growing and may contain precise information about locations of interest to you.

FIRST FAMILIES and PIONEER FAMILIES OF MONROE COUNTY:

The Monroe County Chapter of OGS honors the descendants of families who originally settled this area.

 First Families of Monroe County:

This is an activity of the MCC of OGS that was established in 1993. As of June 2001 there are 92 members and 126 ancestors enrolled. First Families is open to any member of MCC of OGS who is a direct descendent of an individual living in Monroe County by December 31, 1830. An application detailing proof of descent must be submitted with a one-time $15 application fee.

Click here for a printable application to First Families of Monroe County, Ohio

 

Pioneer Families of Monroe County:

This activity does not replace First Families but works in conjunction with it. Pioneer Families is open to any member who is a descendent of an individual who settled in Monroe County by December 31, 1850.

To join Pioneer Families, you must submit an application detailing proof of descent. Include documents providing proof plus a one-time $15.00 application fee. If your family is already enrolled in First Families, then the application fee for Pioneer Families is only $10.00.

Click here for a printable application to Pioneer Families of Monroe County, Ohio

SURNAME FILE:

The Surname File is in transition. At the moment there are two surname files as the transition occurs. A hard-copy Surname File (3" by 5" file cards) is located in the Research Room at the Monroe County District Library. This hard-copy Surname File is in the process of being computerized so that it can be included on-line. New Surnames that are received are published in the MCC of OGS newsletter, the Navigator. Members and non-members should submit their Monroe County surnames either on 3" x 5" cards (one name per card). Each card should include the surname being researched and your name and address. Send your cards to the Monroe Chapter, OGS.

The second Surname File is the computerized list of surnames that appears on this web site. It can be accessed by the link titled 'Surname and Query File' below.
