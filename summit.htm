Annual Summit of the Friends of Freedom Society

The mission of the Friends of Freedom Society is to raise awareness about the lives of African Americans as it relates to the Underground Railroad Movement. To document past events of the Underground Railroad, which was America's first civil rights movement. To preserve and commemorate the cultural landscapes and its connection to the lives of people associated with the Underground Railroad Movement.

Each year the Friends of Freedom Society sponsors a Summit, held in a different Region of Ohio. The Summits have been very successful in increasing understanding of slavery and the Underground Railroad.

On Friday, October 19th, 2001, Saturday, October 20th and Sunday, October 21st, the Sixth Annual Summit will be held at the Muskingum Valley Conference Center at the Holiday Inn in Zanesville, Muskingum County, Ohio. This year's Summit theme is "A Tale of Two Cities".

Each year the Summit gets better! Here you can meet and discuss Ohio's Underground Railroad with professional researchers from all around Ohio and beyond. Also there are discussions about the American Civil War. I encourage teachers and everyone interested in history to attend. For registration forms or more information, please contact the Marietta Area Tourists and Convention Bureau.
