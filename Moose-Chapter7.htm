Chapter Seven    The Mitchell Family11,112-115,164,165

 

Introduction

 

Stephen Ulloms wife was Ellenor Mitchell. Sometimes she is referred to as Nellie and in fact on her grave marker in the Pioneer Cemetery, she is Nellie Ullom wife of S. Ullom. Also, on her request for Stephens pension she gave the name Ellen. On her daughter Rachels death cert her name is Ella Mitchell. There are around 60 family trees on Ancestry.com that cover the Mitchell Family. These trees dont always agree especially as you go back in time. In order to present a reasonable picture of the Mitchell and related families not all of the various branches will be pursued and any contrary information will be shown. All information that can be verified through census and other records will be presented.

 

Elenor Mitchells mother was Eleanor Hill. Eleanor Hill was the daughter of John Hill who died in 1825 in Greene County, PA. Her father was Thomas Mitchell the son of Kent Mitchell and Hannah Wood. Hannah Wood was the daughter of Issac Wood and Elizabeth Wilborn. The Wilborn family was traced to William Welbourne born 11 Oct 1618 in Durham, England. Other surnames are Osborne, Pyngneye, Teague and Parkine. Thomas Parkine born about 1539 in England was Martha Hall Moose 9th Great Grandfather59,60,61.

 

Thomas Mitchell, Martha Halls Great-Grandfather, built the first water wheel mill on Whitten Fork in 1826 in Monroe County. It was a frame building of two stories and 30 feet by 40 feet.  He also built the first saw mill in 1824. He was born in Greene County, PA on 5 March 1772 and he died on 2 June 1852 in Monroe County62. He arrived in Monroe County before 1824. Eleanor Hill Mitchell was born 20 March 1776 and died in Monroe County on 2 July 1848. Like her husband she was buried at the Antioch Church Cemetery in Perry Township.

 

The following advertisement was found in a newspaper article and published in Family Research in Monroe County, Ohio by Catherine Foreaker Fedorchak62.

         

          * Notice  Valuable saw mill for sale- Thomas Mitchell wishes to sell his mill and farm which resides in Jackson Township on Whittens fork of the Little Muskingum and on the road leading from Woodsfield to the Ohio River, 10 miles from the former and 5 miles from the latter. Mill has two roll of stones, the mill house is a large 3 frame, stones are laurelhill burs, sawmill is adjoining the mill and is also in completer operation and is  calculated to cut from 1,000 to 15,000 feet of plank in one day. Farm consists of 80 acres, mostly all bottom land. Log dwelling house and store room, small barn, a good well of water at the door, also a quantity of good bearing fruit trees. Thomas Mitchell

 

From 1836 to 1839 Thomas was a County Commissioner of Monroe County.

Thomas and Eleanor had 11 children, 3 boys and 8 girls53.

 

          Spirit of Democracy, 2 June 1852: Died at the residence of E. Ullom, Esq. in Perry Township on the first inst. in the 81st year of his age, Thomas Mitchell Sr.
